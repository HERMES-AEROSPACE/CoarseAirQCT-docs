O3 System

The O₃ system is a three-body arrangement of three indistinguishable oxygen atoms. It is the largest electronic-manifold system CoarseAIR supports in the adiabatic pipeline, with nine independent electronic surfaces. Input files live under input/O3/.

Arrangement

  • Atoms: O, O, O (all identical).

  • Collision entry: O + O2.

Kinetic processes

  • Inelastic scattering — \(\mathrm{O}_{2}(v, J) + \mathrm{O} \to \mathrm{O}_{2}(v', J') + \mathrm{O}\).

  • Reactive exchange — identical-atom swap; summed to a single channel in indistinguishable mode (see Isotopically Labelled Ozone (O18-O18-O16) for the labelled variant).

  • Dissociation / recombination — \(\mathrm{O}_{2} + \mathrm{O} \leftrightarrow 3\,\mathrm{O}\).

In the adiabatic workflow, each trajectory stays on its assigned surface — nonadiabatic couplings between these surfaces are not modelled. Surface hopping between these nine states is the central feature of CoarseAIR+ (see separate documentation).

PES models

Available functional families (see PES Formats):

  • UMN — analytic MEG expansion, the reference surfaces.

  • NN — neural-network regressions of ab initio points.

  • BNN — Bayesian-NN ensembles (for uncertainty quantification).

  • GP — Gaussian process, three noise-level variants.

  • LEPS — historical benchmark.

All families cover the full set of nine electronic surfaces.

Diatomic partner: O2_UMN (or the variant consistent with the chosen triatomic PES).

Typical use

O3 is relevant to stratospheric chemistry, hypersonic re-entry through oxygen-rich atmospheres (Earth, Mars’ CO2 \(\to\) O2 kinetics), and the ongoing study of the ozone isotope anomaly. It is also the target of the CoarseAIR+ surface-hopping development.
