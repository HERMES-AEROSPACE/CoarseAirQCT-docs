H3 System

The H₃ system is a three-body arrangement of three indistinguishable hydrogen atoms. It is the classical benchmark of QCT literature and is the recommended first example for new users. Input files live under input/H3/.

Arrangement

  • Atoms: H, H, H (all identical).

  • Collision entry: H + H2.

  • All three possible “atom plus diatom” pairings are equivalent by atomic permutation.

Kinetic processes

  • Inelastic scattering — \(\mathrm{H}_{2}(v, J) + \mathrm{H} \to \mathrm{H}_{2}(v', J') + \mathrm{H}\).

  • Reactive exchange — identical-atom exchange: \(\mathrm{H}_a\mathrm{H}_b + \mathrm{H}_c \to \mathrm{H}_a\mathrm{H}_c + \mathrm{H}_b\) etc. In indistinguishable mode, the three outgoing pathways are summed to a single “exchange” channel.

  • Dissociation / recombination — \(\mathrm{H}_{2} + \mathrm{H} \leftrightarrow 3\,\mathrm{H}\).

Because all three atoms are identical, H3 is the ideal test of CoarseAIR’s indistinguishable-atom accounting. A labelled-atom run (all three H atoms treated as distinguishable) can be compared against the indistinguishable result as a self-consistency check.

PES models

Available: BH (Boothroyd–Keogh–Martin–Peterson / BKMP2 variant), LEPS (historical).

Diatomic partner: H2_Schwenke1988.

Typical use

H + H2 has been computed by countless QCT codes since the 1970s; reference cross-sections and rates exist in the literature across a wide temperature range. It is the first system any serious QCT validation exercise should run, and CoarseAIR’s H3 numbers should be compared against the body of published results before running production studies on chemically heavier systems.
