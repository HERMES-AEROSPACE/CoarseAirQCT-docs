CNO System

The CNO system is a three-body arrangement of three distinguishable atoms: carbon, nitrogen, oxygen. Input files live under input/CNO/.

Arrangement

  • Atoms: C, N, O (all distinguishable).

  • Collision entries: CN + O, CO + N, or NO + C.

  • Atom-pair numbering: \((A, B, C) = (C, N, O)\) → pair 1 = CN, pair 2 = CO, pair 3 = NO.

Kinetic processes

Three distinct diatomic products are possible, connected via the OCN / NCO / CON intermediates on the PES:

  • Inelastic scattering — on the incoming arrangement.

  • Reactive exchange among the three channels:

    \[\mathrm{CN} + \mathrm{O} \;\leftrightarrow\; \mathrm{CO} + \mathrm{N} \;\leftrightarrow\; \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{C}.\]

  • Dissociation — C + N + O at high energy.

Multi-surface structure

CNO has three low-lying doublet/quartet electronic surfaces that are run as independent adiabatic PES in CoarseAIR:

  • \({}^{2}A'\) (UCI_2A1)

  • \({}^{2}A''\) (UCI_2A2)

  • \({}^{4}A''\) (UCI_4A2)

Trajectories are distributed across these surfaces proportionally to their electronic degeneracies (see Sampling). Results are combined at post-processing to produce degeneracy-weighted state-to-state rates.

Nonadiabatic couplings between these surfaces — which would modify the resulting kinetics via surface hopping — are handled in CoarseAIR+, not in the adiabatic workflow.

PES models

Available surfaces: UCI_2A1, UCI_2A2, UCI_4A2 (UC Irvine group — see Bibliography for the three DMBE-surface DOIs), Basel. Example inputs target thermal rate coefficients around \(T \sim 10\,000\) K.

Diatomic partners: CN_UCI, CO_UCI, NO_UCI (or the Basel counterparts when the Basel PES is selected).
