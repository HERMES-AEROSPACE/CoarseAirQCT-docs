Rate & Cross-Section Extraction
===============================

After trajectory integration and final-state classification, CoarseAIR
converts the raw "which-process-happened" counters into statistically
well-defined cross-sections and rate coefficients. This chapter derives
the estimators the code uses, their variance, and the thermal-averaging
procedure that produces the final :math:`k(T)`.

The implementation is in ``src/Processes/`` (per-process accumulators)
and in the ``TrajectoriesStats`` / ``PostTrajectories`` application
stages.


Cross-section estimator
-----------------------

Consider a specific initial state :math:`(v, J)` on a specific PES at a
fixed collision energy :math:`E`. Suppose we ran :math:`N_i` trajectories
at each impact-parameter bin :math:`i` (with annulus
:math:`[b_{i-1}, b_i]`), and :math:`N_{iJ}` of them produced the target
process :math:`J` — an inelastic transition to a specific :math:`(v', J')`,
an exchange channel, a dissociation outcome, etc.

Within bin :math:`i` the probability of process :math:`J` is estimated
by the Bernoulli proportion

.. math::

   \hat{P}_{iJ} \;=\; \frac{N_{iJ}}{N_i}.

Writing the cross-section as an integral over the impact-parameter plane,

.. math::

   \sigma_J
      \;=\; \int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^\infty
            \tilde{P}_J(b, \theta)\, b\, db\, d\theta
      \;\approx\; \sum_i A_{\text{ring},i}\, \hat{P}_{iJ},

with annulus area :math:`A_{\text{ring},i} = \pi (b_i^2 - b_{i-1}^2)`. The
CoarseAIR estimator is therefore

.. math::

   \boxed{\;
   \hat{\sigma}_J \;=\; \sum_{i} \pi \left(b_i^2 - b_{i-1}^2\right)
                        \frac{N_{iJ}}{N_i}
   \;}

with :math:`b_0 = 0`. The summation runs over all impact-parameter bins
up to :math:`b_{\max}` (see :doc:`sampling`).


Variance and uncertainty
------------------------

Because each trajectory in bin :math:`i` is an independent Bernoulli trial
with success probability :math:`P_{iJ}`, the count :math:`N_{iJ}` has
binomial variance :math:`N_i\, P_{iJ}\,(1 - P_{iJ})`. Propagating to the
cross-section estimator,

.. math::

   \operatorname{Var}(\hat{\sigma}_J)
     \;=\; \sum_i A_{\text{ring},i}^{2}\,
           \frac{\hat{P}_{iJ}(1 - \hat{P}_{iJ})}{N_i},

so the standard deviation is

.. math::

   \boxed{\;
   \operatorname{SD}(\hat{\sigma}_J)
     \;=\; \sqrt{\sum_i A_{\text{ring},i}^{2}\,
                 \frac{\hat{P}_{iJ}\,(1 - \hat{P}_{iJ})}{N_i}}
   \;}

The relative statistical error of a rare event scales as
:math:`1/\sqrt{N\, P_J}`, so low-probability channels (e.g. dissociation
at low :math:`T`) require many trajectories to reach tolerable error
bars. This is a primary reason for stratified impact-parameter sampling
(see :doc:`sampling`) — concentrating trajectories at small :math:`b`,
where reaction is more likely, reduces the variance at fixed cost.

For two *complementary* processes :math:`J` and :math:`J'`
(e.g. "exchange" versus "everything else"), :math:`N_{iJ} + N_{iJ'} = N_i`
gives the simplified form

.. math::

   \operatorname{SD}(\hat{\sigma}_J)
     \;=\; \sqrt{\sum_i \frac{\hat{\sigma}_{iJ}\, \hat{\sigma}_{iJ'}}{N_i}},

used as a cross-check inside the code.


Thermal rate coefficient
------------------------

The thermal rate constant at temperature :math:`T` is the
Maxwell–Boltzmann average

.. math::

   k(T) \;=\; \left\langle \sigma\, w \right\rangle
          \;=\; 4\pi \int_0^\infty \sigma(w)\, f(w)\, w^3\, dw,
   \qquad
   f(w) \;=\; \left(\frac{m_e}{2\pi k_B T}\right)^{3/2}
              \exp\!\left(-\frac{m_e w^2}{2 k_B T}\right),

where :math:`m_e` is the reduced mass of the collision.

If the user runs trajectories at a fixed grid of collision energies,
CoarseAIR performs the integral numerically (Gauss-Legendre quadrature,
``Tools/GaussQuadrature``) on :math:`E = \tfrac{1}{2} m_e w^2`.

If the user runs trajectories in **thermal mode**, collision energies are
drawn directly from the Maxwellian via inverse-CDF sampling (see
:doc:`sampling`). Substituting :math:`E = k_B T\, \hat{E}`, the rate
collapses to a simple Monte Carlo average:

.. math::

   \boxed{\;
   k(T) \;\approx\;
     \sqrt{\frac{8 k_B T}{\mu \pi}}\;
     \frac{1}{N}\sum_{p=1}^{N} \sigma\!\left(\hat{E}_p\right)
   \;}

where :math:`N` is the total number of trajectories at that temperature.
No quadrature is needed — the energy distribution is built into the
sampling itself.

This is the formula used by ``PostTrajectories`` to produce the final
:math:`k(T)` tables from a thermal run.


State-to-state, grouped, and Arrhenius outputs
----------------------------------------------

CoarseAIR produces three levels of coarse-grained output; see
:doc:`/user/postprocessing` for the file layout.

- **State-to-state (StS).** One rate per pair of levels:
  :math:`k_{(v, J) \to (v', J')}(T)`. Largest output; suitable for direct
  use in state-to-state master-equation solvers
  (:doc:`/reference/about` → dmsAIR).
- **Grouped rates.** Rates are summed into bins defined by the binning
  strategy used at input (vibrational, energy-based, or user-provided
  groups). Typically :math:`10^3`–:math:`10^4` rates per temperature.
- **Arrhenius fits.** Effective three-parameter :math:`A, n, E_a` fits
  in the form

  .. math::

     k(T) \;=\; A\, T^{n}\, \exp(-E_a / k_B T),

  are generated per process and stored in the ``Process%ArrCoeffs(3)``
  field. Useful for multiscale solvers where the full temperature
  dependence is needed in closed form.

Error bars propagate consistently across all three aggregations — the
binomial variance from the cross-section stage is carried through
grouping and summation by standard Gaussian error propagation.


Complementarity and mass-balance checks
---------------------------------------

CoarseAIR exploits two self-consistency checks that users should always
inspect before trusting a run:

1. **Sum rule across outcomes** — for every starting level, the
   cross-sections for all outgoing channels (elastic + inelastic +
   exchange + dissociation) should sum to :math:`\pi b_{\max}^2` within
   statistical error. Deviations flag either a too-small :math:`b_{\max}`
   (reactive cross-section is cut off) or a classification bug.

2. **Detailed balance** — for a pair of states :math:`(v, J)` and
   :math:`(v', J')` at the same temperature,

   .. math::

      g_{vJ}\, k_{vJ \to v'J'}(T)\,
      \exp\!\left(-E_{vJ}/k_B T\right)
      \;=\;
      g_{v'J'}\, k_{v'J' \to vJ}(T)\,
      \exp\!\left(-E_{v'J'}/k_B T\right),

   where :math:`g` are the rotational degeneracies. Violations are
   expected within the QCT statistical error; persistent systematic
   violations (>2–3 σ) point at zero-point-energy artefacts or
   classification errors.

Both checks are available as diagnostics in the post-processing Python
and MATLAB tooling.


Derivations and further detail
------------------------------

**Bernoulli variance derivation.** Each of the :math:`N_i`
trajectories in impact-parameter bin :math:`i` is an independent
Bernoulli trial with success probability :math:`P_{iJ}` (the
trajectory either produces process :math:`J` or it doesn't). The
number of successes :math:`N_{iJ}` therefore follows a binomial
distribution with mean :math:`N_i P_{iJ}` and variance
:math:`N_i P_{iJ}(1 - P_{iJ})`. Writing the cross-section estimator
as the linear combination
:math:`\hat\sigma_J = \sum_i A_{\text{ring},i}\,N_{iJ}/N_i` and
using independence across bins,

.. math::

   \operatorname{Var}(\hat\sigma_J)
   \;=\; \sum_i A_{\text{ring},i}^{2}\,
         \frac{P_{iJ}(1 - P_{iJ})}{N_i}.

Substituting the sample proportion
:math:`\hat P_{iJ} = N_{iJ}/N_i` gives the standard-deviation
estimator used by CoarseAIR's rate post-processing. The formula
assumes strictly independent trajectories — impact parameters
drawn independently from the sampling distribution, with no shared
random draws between trajectories. CoarseAIR's mini-batch-parallel
execution preserves this independence because each mini-batch
re-seeds from the master RNG stream.

**Complementary-process correlation.** If processes :math:`J`
and :math:`J'` are complementary (:math:`N_{iJ} + N_{iJ'} = N_i`),
then :math:`P_{iJ} + P_{iJ'} = 1` exactly, and the binomial variance
can be rewritten as
:math:`\operatorname{Var}(N_{iJ}/N_i) = \hat P_{iJ}\hat P_{iJ'}/N_i`.
Summing over bins gives the compact cross-check

.. math::

   \operatorname{SD}(\hat\sigma_J)
   \;=\; \sqrt{\sum_i \frac{\hat\sigma_{iJ}\,\hat\sigma_{iJ'}}{N_i}}.

**Maxwell-Boltzmann change of variables.** Starting from
:math:`k(T) = \int d^{3}\mathbf{w}\,f(\mathbf{w})\,|\mathbf{w}|\,
\sigma(|\mathbf{w}|)` with isotropic Maxwellian
:math:`f(\mathbf{w}) = (\mu/2\pi k_B T)^{3/2} \exp(-\mu w^{2}/2k_B T)`,
passing to spherical coordinates and substituting
:math:`E = \tfrac{1}{2}\mu w^{2}`, :math:`\hat E = E/k_B T` gives

.. math::

   k(T) \;=\; \sqrt{\frac{8 k_B T}{\mu\pi}}\,
              \int_0^\infty \hat E\,e^{-\hat E}\,\sigma(\hat E)\,d\hat E.

When the Monte Carlo sampler draws :math:`\hat E` directly from
:math:`\hat E\,e^{-\hat E}` via the inverse-CDF procedure (see
:doc:`sampling`), the integral collapses to an arithmetic mean of
:math:`\sigma(\hat E)` over the drawn sample — the formula used by
``coarseair-posttrajectories.x`` in thermal mode.

**Detailed balance across levels.** For an ideal gas in
thermodynamic equilibrium, microscopic reversibility of collisions
imposes

.. math::

   g_{vJ}\,k_{vJ\to v'J'}(T)\,e^{-E_{vJ}/k_B T}
   \;=\;
   g_{v'J'}\,k_{v'J'\to vJ}(T)\,e^{-E_{v'J'}/k_B T}

on the ensemble-averaged rates (not the individual trajectories).
Systematic violations well outside the Monte Carlo statistical
error (more than 2–3 standard deviations) indicate either a
classification bug or a QCT artefact such as zero-point-energy
leakage — the single most sensitive diagnostic for production
rate datasets.

**Arrhenius fit.** For each process :math:`d`, CoarseAIR fits

.. math::

   \ln k(T) \;=\; \ln A \;+\; n\,\ln T \;-\; \frac{E_a}{k_B T}

by unweighted least squares over the sampled temperatures,
producing :math:`(A, n, E_a)` stored in ``Process%ArrCoeffs``.
Extrapolation far outside the sampled temperature range is
unreliable — the pre-exponential :math:`n` is especially sensitive
to the sampled range. For multi-regime behaviour a piecewise or
modified-Arrhenius form is more appropriate; that fit is not
produced automatically.
