Level Generation (Numerov method)
=================================

Before any trajectory can be run, CoarseAIR needs the ro-vibrational
energy-level table of every diatomic molecule that may appear as a
reactant or product. The table ships pre-computed for most supported
species (see ``dtb/Molecules/<Species>/``), but for new potentials or
new PES variants the levels must be recomputed. This chapter describes
the numerical machinery behind that computation.

The relevant code lives in ``src/Levels/LevelsGenerator_Module.F90``
and is called by the executable ``coarseair-computelevels.x`` whenever
``Generating Energy Levels? = yes`` in the input.


What is being solved
--------------------

For each diatomic molecule with internuclear distance :math:`R` and
reduced mass :math:`\mu`, CoarseAIR solves the **radial Schrödinger
equation** for the bound ro-vibrational states of the effective
potential

.. math::

   V_{\text{eff}}(R; J) \;=\; V_{\text{diat}}(R)
                             \;+\; \frac{\left(J + \tfrac{1}{2}\right)^{2}}{2\mu R^{2}},

where :math:`V_{\text{diat}}(R)` is the chosen diatomic potential (see
:doc:`pes_formats`) and the centrifugal term uses the Langer
correction :math:`J(J+1) \to (J + \tfrac{1}{2})^{2}`. The goal is to
find every eigenpair :math:`\{E_{vJ}, \psi_{vJ}(R)\}` such that

.. math::

   -\frac{1}{2\mu}\frac{d^2 \psi_{vJ}}{dR^2}
   \;+\; V_{\text{eff}}(R; J)\, \psi_{vJ}
   \;=\; E_{vJ}\, \psi_{vJ},

with :math:`\psi_{vJ}(R)` satisfying the usual bound-state boundary
conditions at :math:`R \to 0` and :math:`R \to \infty`.


Numerov integration
-------------------

CoarseAIR integrates the radial Schrödinger equation with the
**Numerov method** — a three-point recurrence specialised to equations
of the form :math:`\psi''(R) = f(R)\, \psi(R)`. Writing
:math:`\psi_n = \psi(R_n)` on a uniform grid of spacing :math:`h`, the
update is

.. math::

   \left(1 - \tfrac{h^2}{12} f_{n+1}\right) \psi_{n+1}
   \;=\; 2\left(1 + \tfrac{5 h^2}{12} f_n\right) \psi_n
      \;-\; \left(1 - \tfrac{h^2}{12} f_{n-1}\right) \psi_{n-1},

with

.. math::

   f(R) \;=\; 2\mu\left[\, V_{\text{eff}}(R; J) - E \,\right].

The scheme is fourth-order accurate and has excellent stability
properties for oscillatory bound-state wavefunctions. For the
canonical reference see *Sandvik (2016)* "Numerical Solutions of the
Schrödinger Equation" and, for the coupled-channel generalisation,
Johnson's *J. Chem. Phys.* **69**, 4678 (1978) — both cited in
:doc:`/reference/bibliography`.


Eigenvalue search
-----------------

For each :math:`J` the bound-state eigenvalues :math:`E_{vJ}` are
found by a two-stage bracketing:

1. **Bisection** (40 iterations by default) narrows the interval
   :math:`[V_{\min}, V_\infty]` — the potential well plus any
   centrifugal barrier — to a small bracket around each eigenvalue
   using sign changes in the logarithmic derivative of
   :math:`\psi` (the number-of-nodes criterion).
2. **Newton–Raphson refinement** (80 iterations) polishes each
   eigenvalue to high precision using a derivative-based update.

Both stages call the Numerov forward/backward integrators and a
matching condition at the classical turning point. The logic is
implemented in ``LevelsGenerator_Module.F90``.


Semi-classical action integrals
-------------------------------

In addition to :math:`E_{vJ}`, CoarseAIR computes the **WKB action
integral**

.. math::

   S(E, J) \;=\; \oint p(R)\, dR
              \;=\; 2 \int_{R_{\text{in}}}^{R_{\text{out}}}
                    \sqrt{2\mu\,[E - V_{\text{eff}}(R; J)]}\, dR,

between the inner and outer classical turning points. At each computed
eigenvalue this should give :math:`S \approx 2\pi\hbar\, (v +
\tfrac{1}{2})`; deviations are used as a quality check. The action
integral is the same quantity used at run time to quantise the final
vibrational state of each trajectory — see
:doc:`arrangement_classification`.

The action is evaluated by Gauss–Legendre quadrature; the number of
quadrature points is set in the input file
(``Nb of Quadrature Pts for Action Integral =``, typically 50).


Bound, quasi-bound, unbound
---------------------------

For each :math:`J`, three kinds of solutions can appear:

- **Bound** — :math:`E_{vJ} < V_\infty` (:math:`V_\infty` = the
  asymptotic potential). The standard case.
- **Quasi-bound** — :math:`V_\infty < E_{vJ} < V_{\max}(J)` where
  :math:`V_{\max}(J)` is the centrifugal-barrier maximum. These states
  are metastable — classically trapped behind the barrier but
  asymptotically unbound. CoarseAIR retains them in the level table
  with a finite lifetime :math:`\Gamma_{vJ}` (column 4 of the output
  file) computed from the barrier transmission.
- **Unbound** — :math:`E_{vJ} > V_{\max}(J)`. Discarded.

The quasi-bound set matters physically because a significant fraction
of near-dissociation population at high temperature sits in these
levels. CoarseAIR's level generator treats them as genuine levels;
downstream post-processing can choose to include or exclude them
(see :doc:`arrangement_classification`).


Turning-point and extremum search
---------------------------------

The Numerov solver needs accurate turning points
:math:`R_{\text{in}}, R_{\text{out}}` and, for quasi-bound levels, the
position and height of the centrifugal-barrier maximum. The diatomic
potential object (``DiatomicPotential_Type``) exposes
``FindMinimum``, ``FindMaximum``, and ``TurningPoint`` methods that
locate these by bracketed root-finding on :math:`V_{\text{eff}}(R; J)`.

The CoarseAIR input lets you prescribe the search window for the
barrier maximum:

.. code-block:: text

   Lower Bound for Position of Centrifugal Barrier Max = 1.0d0
   Upper Bound for Position of Centrifugal Barrier Max = 30.d0

Getting this wrong (barrier outside the search window) is a common
cause of "missing" quasi-bound levels; widen the window if the level
table looks truncated in :math:`J`.


Output level file
-----------------

The per-molecule level table written by ``coarseair-computelevels.x``
lives at ``<rundir>/Test/<System>/<Molecule>/<filename>`` where
``<filename>`` comes from ``GenerateLevels.inp``. Each row is one level
with 11 columns:

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 6 22 72

   * - #
     - Column
     - Meaning
   * - 1
     - :math:`v`
     - Vibrational quantum number.
   * - 2
     - :math:`J`
     - Rotational quantum number.
   * - 3
     - :math:`E` (Eh)
     - Internal energy.
   * - 4
     - :math:`\Gamma`
     - Lifetime parameter for quasi-bound levels.
   * - 5
     - :math:`R_{V_{\min}(J)}` (a.u.)
     - Position of the effective-potential minimum.
   * - 6
     - :math:`R_{V_{\max}(J)}` (a.u.)
     - Position of the centrifugal barrier.
   * - 7
     - :math:`V^{\min}(J)` (Eh)
     - Well depth of the effective potential.
   * - 8
     - :math:`V^{\max}(J)` (Eh)
     - Height of the centrifugal barrier.
   * - 9
     - :math:`\tau_v` (a.u.)
     - Vibrational period.
   * - 10
     - :math:`R_{\text{in}}` (:math:`a_0`)
     - Inner turning point.
   * - 11
     - :math:`R_{\text{out}}` (:math:`a_0`)
     - Outer turning point.

The same level file is consumed later by the trajectory initial-state
sampler (see :doc:`sampling`) and by the final-state quantiser (see
:doc:`arrangement_classification`).


What this replaces in a QCT pipeline
------------------------------------

Some older QCT codes compute ro-vibrational actions on the fly during
each trajectory's final-state analysis. CoarseAIR does all of that
work once, up front, at machine-precision for every :math:`(v, J)` it
will ever need. This choice is what enables:

- Efficient histogram binning of final trajectory outcomes without
  having to re-solve the Schrödinger equation in the hot path.
- Clean separation between PES work (level generation) and dynamics
  work (trajectories), so the two can be debugged and validated
  independently.
- Fast switching between binning strategies in post-processing — once
  levels exist, regrouping into state-specific, vibration-specific, or
  coarse-grained bins is essentially free.
