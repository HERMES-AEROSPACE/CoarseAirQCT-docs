Sampling
========

Each trajectory needs six pieces of information before integration can start:
the initial ro-vibrational state :math:`(v, J)` of the target diatom, the
collision energy :math:`E`, the impact parameter :math:`b`, three orientation
Euler angles, and the electronic-surface index ``iPES``. This chapter
describes how CoarseAIR samples each of them.

The relevant code lives under ``src/Collision/InitialStates/`` —
specifically ``StateInitDiatomAtom_Module.F90`` (3-body) and
``StateInitDiatomDiatom_Module.F90`` (4-body).


Ro-vibrational state :math:`(v, J)`
-----------------------------------

Internal states come from the pre-computed level database
(``dtb/Molecules/<species>/Bins_*/levels_cut.inp``). CoarseAIR offers four
selection strategies, chosen by the ``Bins/Nb of PESs`` and related input
blocks and implemented in ``src/BinnedMolecules/``:

- **State-to-State (StS)** — one bin per :math:`(v, J)` level. This gives
  full state-resolved output but is expensive at high temperature.
- **Vibrational Specific (VibSpec)** — bins are labelled by :math:`v` only;
  rotational sub-levels within each :math:`v` are distributed by
  their Boltzmann weight. Standard for vibrational-ladder studies.
- **Energy-based** — bins group levels within internal-energy windows. Used
  for coarse-grained models (group-specific rates).
- **Hybrid / FromFile** — user-supplied partitioning, read directly from a
  file listing levels per bin.

Within a selected bin, the specific starting level is drawn with probability
proportional to its degeneracy-weighted Boltzmann factor.


Collision energy :math:`E`
--------------------------

CoarseAIR provides four *translational-energy-model* options,
selected via ``Relative Translational Energy Model =`` in the input
file (see :doc:`/user/input_reference` for the exact syntax of
each):

.. list-table::
   :header-rows: 1
   :widths: 18 82

   * - Keyword
     - Energy distribution
   * - ``FixedRelE``
     - **Delta-function** :math:`\delta(E - E_{\text{rel}})`: every
       trajectory starts at the same specified energy.
   * - ``Boltzmann``
     - **Maxwell–Boltzmann** collision-rate-weighted density
       :math:`\hat{E}\,e^{-\hat{E}}` at temperature :math:`T`
       (see below).
   * - ``Gaussian``
     - **Gaussian** :math:`\mathcal{N}(\mu_E, \sigma_E^2)` with
       user-specified mean and standard deviation.
   * - ``FixedTotEn``
     - **Delta-function on the total energy**
       :math:`E_{\text{tot}} = E_{\text{rel}} + E_{\text{int}}`.
       Useful for holding :math:`E_{\text{tot}}` fixed while the
       ro-vibrational partition varies.

Each option feeds different trajectories to the same downstream
dynamics; the choice is entirely one of *what statistical ensemble
is being sampled*.

**FixedRelE (delta-function sampling).** Every trajectory starts at
the same :math:`E_{\text{rel}}` — no variance from the energy
distribution. The resulting cross-section estimator is
:math:`\hat\sigma_J(E_{\text{rel}})`, and by running on a grid of
energies one builds up the full excitation function
:math:`\sigma_J(E)`. Thermal averaging can then be done offline via
numerical quadrature. This is the mode of choice when the user wants
transparent control of what energies are being probed.

**Boltzmann (Maxwell–Boltzmann thermal sampling).** Energies are
drawn from the translational Maxwellian at temperature :math:`T`.
Starting from

.. math::

   k(T) \;=\; 4\pi \!\int_0^\infty \sigma(w)\, f(w)\, w^3\, dw,
   \qquad
   f(w) \;=\; \left(\frac{\mu}{2\pi k_B T}\right)^{3/2}
              \exp\!\left(-\frac{\mu w^{2}}{2 k_B T}\right),

the change of variables to reduced energy
:math:`\hat{E} = E/k_B T` gives the collision-rate-weighted density

.. math::

   p(\hat{E}) \;=\; \hat{E}\, e^{-\hat{E}},
   \qquad
   F(\hat{E}) \;=\; 1 \;-\; e^{-\hat{E}}\,(\hat{E} + 1).

CoarseAIR draws :math:`U \sim \mathcal{U}[0,1]`, inverts the CDF by
Newton–Raphson to obtain :math:`\hat{E}`, and sets
:math:`E = \hat{E}\, k_B T`. The thermal rate then collapses to the
arithmetic mean

.. math::

   k(T) \;\approx\; \sqrt{\frac{8 k_B T}{\mu\pi}}\;
                    \frac{1}{N}\sum_{p = 1}^{N} \sigma(\hat{E}_p).

**Gaussian sampling.** Energies are drawn from
:math:`\mathcal{N}(\mu_E, \sigma_E^2)` with user-specified mean
and standard deviation. The resulting rate is the numerical
Gaussian average

.. math::

   \bar\sigma \;=\; \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}
                    \sigma(E)\,
                    \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\,\sigma_E}
                    \exp\!\left[
                      -\frac{(E - \mu_E)^{2}}{2\sigma_E^{2}}
                    \right] dE,

estimated in practice by a sample mean over the drawn energies.
Unphysical negative energy draws (always possible for a Gaussian)
are resampled. The mode is most useful to reproduce narrow
non-thermal distributions from upstream simulation data (laser
excitation, shock-compressed gas) where neither a Maxwellian nor a
single fixed energy is a good model.

**FixedTotEn.** Energies are drawn such that the *total* energy
(translational + internal) is fixed at
:math:`E_{\text{tot}}`. Given the sampled initial ro-vibrational
state with internal energy :math:`E_{\text{int}}(v, J)`,
CoarseAIR sets :math:`E_{\text{rel}} = E_{\text{tot}} -
E_{\text{int}}(v, J)` for that trajectory. This keeps the total
energy constant as the :math:`(v, J)` partition varies — a useful
probe for vibrational-threshold studies and for comparing
state-specific reactivity at matched collision energies.

All four sampling code paths live in ``src/Random/`` and the
initial-state modules under ``src/Collision/InitialStates/``;
the branching on ``This%TtraModel`` is in
``src/Input_Class.F90``.


Impact parameter :math:`b`
--------------------------

By standard kinematics, the differential cross-section in :math:`b` carries
weight :math:`2\pi b\,db`, so uniform sampling *in* :math:`b^2` over
:math:`[0, b_{\max}^2]` reproduces the correct weighting without further
Jacobians. CoarseAIR's input offers four impact-parameter models:

- ``Normal`` — uniform in :math:`b^2`, single :math:`b_{\max}`. Simplest and
  default for most dissociation / inelastic studies.
- ``Random`` — uniform :math:`b`, requires Jacobian reweighting during rate
  extraction. Mainly for diagnostic use.
- ``Stratified`` — :math:`b` is split into concentric annuli
  :math:`[b_{i-1}, b_i]` with pre-assigned trajectory counts :math:`N_i`.
  Reduces variance at small :math:`b` where reaction is most likely.
- ``FixedAngMom`` — :math:`b` is chosen to reproduce a target orbital
  angular momentum, useful for reactive-scattering studies at specified
  :math:`\ell`.

The value of :math:`b_{\max}` is system- and energy-dependent; a brief
pre-run "scattering map" (see :doc:`/examples/h3`) establishes the smallest
:math:`b` at which *no* reactive/inelastic events occur, and that value sets
:math:`b_{\max}`.


Relative-velocity decomposition
-------------------------------

Given :math:`E`, :math:`b`, and the initial separation :math:`D_{\text{init}}`
along the :math:`x` axis in the body-fixed frame, the relative velocity
magnitude and components are

.. math::

   V_{e_R} \;=\; \sqrt{\frac{2 E}{\mu}}, \qquad
   V_{e_z} \;=\; -V_{e_R}\,\frac{b}{D_{\text{init}}}, \qquad
   V_{e_x} \;=\; -V_{e_R}\,\sqrt{1 - \frac{b^2}{D_{\text{init}}^2}}.

The minus signs encode the convention that the projectile approaches the
target (which sits at the origin). :math:`V_{e_y} = 0` is imposed by
placing the collision plane to coincide with :math:`x`–:math:`z`.

The projectile's initial Cartesian position and velocity vectors are then
split between target and projectile reduced-mass fractions:

.. math::

   \mathbf{R}_T \;=\; -\frac{m_P}{m_T + m_P}\, D_{\text{init}}\, \hat{\mathbf{x}},
   \qquad
   \mathbf{R}_P \;=\; +\frac{m_T}{m_T + m_P}\, D_{\text{init}}\, \hat{\mathbf{x}}.


Orientation sampling (Euler angles)
-----------------------------------

The target diatom's orientation in the body-fixed frame is randomised by
three successive Euler rotations. Using the standard :math:`z`-:math:`y`-
:math:`z` convention :math:`(\alpha, \beta, \gamma)`:

.. math::

   \begin{pmatrix} x'\\ y'\\ z' \end{pmatrix}
   \;=\;
   R_z(\gamma)\, R_y(\beta)\, R_z(\alpha)
   \begin{pmatrix} x\\ y\\ z \end{pmatrix}.

With :math:`\alpha, \gamma` uniform on :math:`[0, 2\pi)` and
:math:`\cos\beta` uniform on :math:`[-1, 1]` (to sample the sphere
uniformly), the resulting orientation is isotropic. The angles are drawn
independently per trajectory. For 4-atom systems the same scheme is applied
to both colliding diatoms independently.


Electronic-surface index ``iPES``
---------------------------------

When multiple electronic surfaces are active (e.g. O\ :sub:`3` with 9
surfaces, CNO with 3, CO\ :sub:`2` with 5), CoarseAIR distributes
trajectories across surfaces proportionally to their electronic
degeneracies.

At initialisation, the routine ``SetPESIndx()`` (called from the collision
class) assigns each trajectory an ``iPES`` drawn from the discrete
distribution

.. math::

   P(\text{iPES} = s) \;=\; \frac{g_s}{\sum_{s'} g_{s'}},

where :math:`g_s` is the degeneracy of surface :math:`s` specified in the
input file. The per-PES trajectory counter
``Traj%iTrajPES(1:NPESs)`` tracks how many trajectories have been assigned
to each surface, so the final statistics (cross-sections, rates) are
weighted consistently.

``iPES(iTraj)`` never changes after this initial assignment — see
:doc:`pes_formats` for the multi-surface machinery.


Derivations and further detail
------------------------------

**Why uniform in** :math:`b^2`. The differential reactive cross-section
at impact parameter :math:`b` is, by definition, :math:`2\pi b\,P(b)\,db`
where :math:`P(b)` is the reaction probability. Monte Carlo estimation
of :math:`\sigma = \int 2\pi b\,P(b)\,db` has minimum variance when
the sampling density matches :math:`2\pi b`, i.e. when :math:`b` is
drawn with PDF proportional to :math:`b`. Substituting
:math:`u = b^2`, :math:`du = 2b\,db`, this becomes a *uniform*
distribution on :math:`u \in [0, b_{\max}^2]`. Equivalently, to
generate such a :math:`b`, draw :math:`U \sim \mathcal{U}[0,1]` and
set :math:`b = b_{\max}\sqrt{U}`. CoarseAIR's ``Normal`` impact-
parameter model does exactly this; the ``Random`` model by contrast
samples :math:`b` uniformly and requires an explicit Jacobian
reweighting downstream.

**Stratified sampling variance reduction.** Splitting the domain
:math:`[0, b_{\max}^2]` into :math:`K` disjoint strata of fractional
weight :math:`w_i` and allocating :math:`N_i` trajectories to each
stratum gives the estimator
:math:`\hat\sigma = \pi b_{\max}^2 \sum_i w_i\, \hat P_i` with
variance

.. math::

   \operatorname{Var}(\hat\sigma)
   \;=\; (\pi b_{\max}^{2})^{2}\,
         \sum_{i=1}^{K} \frac{w_i^{2}\,P_i(1 - P_i)}{N_i}.

This is strictly smaller than the unstratified variance whenever the
:math:`P_i` differ across strata — the usual case, since reaction
probability drops steeply with :math:`b`. The optimal Neyman
allocation :math:`N_i \propto w_i\sqrt{P_i(1-P_i)}` is approximated
in practice by allocating roughly twice as many trajectories to
small-:math:`b` strata as to large-:math:`b` ones; the exact
per-stratum counts are a user choice in ``CoarseAIR.inp``.

**Inverse-CDF sampling derivation.** Starting from the 3-D
Maxwellian speed density
:math:`f(w)\,4\pi w^{2}\,dw` with :math:`f(w) = (m/2\pi k_B T)^{3/2}
\exp(-m w^{2}/2k_B T)`, the substitution :math:`E = \tfrac{1}{2}m w^{2}`
(so :math:`w\,dw = dE/m`) gives the *collision-rate weighted* energy
density

.. math::

   p(E)\,dE \;=\;
   \frac{1}{(k_B T)^{2}}\,E\,e^{-E/k_B T}\,dE
   \;=\; \hat{E}\,e^{-\hat{E}}\,d\hat{E},

where :math:`\hat{E} = E/k_B T`. Its cumulative distribution
:math:`F(\hat{E}) = 1 - (1 + \hat{E})\,e^{-\hat{E}}` does not admit a
closed-form inverse, so CoarseAIR solves :math:`F(\hat{E}) = U` with
:math:`U\sim\mathcal{U}[0,1]` by Newton–Raphson iteration: starting
from the initial guess :math:`\hat{E}_0 = -\ln(1-U)` (which is
exact if :math:`p = e^{-\hat{E}}` were used in place of the
collision-weighted density), the update

.. math::

   \hat{E}_{n+1} \;=\; \hat{E}_{n} \;-\;
     \frac{F(\hat{E}_n) - U}{\hat{E}_n\,e^{-\hat{E}_n}}

converges quadratically in about 5 iterations to :math:`10^{-12}`
relative accuracy. The code lives in the RNG utilities under
``src/Random/``.

**Isotropy of the Euler sampling.** Uniform :math:`\alpha, \gamma
\in [0, 2\pi)` with :math:`\cos\beta \sim \mathcal{U}[-1, 1]`
produces an SO(3)-invariant distribution of molecular
orientations. A quick way to see this: the Haar measure on SO(3),
expressed in Euler angles, is :math:`dg = \sin\beta\,d\alpha\,d\beta\,
d\gamma / (8\pi^2)`. Changing variables to :math:`u = \cos\beta`
gives :math:`dg = du\,d\alpha\,d\gamma / (8\pi^2)`, so the product
measure of uniform :math:`\alpha, \gamma` and uniform :math:`u`
reproduces Haar — i.e. isotropic orientation. This matters for
getting the correct unweighted average of reactive cross-sections
over molecular orientation.

**Proportional vs equal allocation.** With ``Using Proportional
Allocation? = yes``, the total trajectory budget
:math:`N_\text{tot}` is distributed across levels proportionally
to their Boltzmann weights :math:`g_i e^{-E_i/k_B T_\text{int}}`.
This concentrates trajectories where the population is actually
large — efficient for thermal rate coefficients but wasteful for
state-to-state studies where every level needs tight error bars.
With ``Using Proportional Allocation? = no`` and ``Nb of
Trajectories per Level / Bin`` specified, every level receives the
same trajectory count. Both modes produce statistically consistent
thermal rates; the choice is a variance-reduction question, not a
correctness one.
