Sampling
========

Each trajectory needs six pieces of information before integration can start:
the initial ro-vibrational state :math:`(v, J)` of the target diatom, the
collision energy :math:`E`, the impact parameter :math:`b`, three orientation
Euler angles, and the electronic-surface index ``iPES``. This chapter
describes how CoarseAIR samples each of them.

The relevant code lives under ``src/Collision/InitialStates/`` —
specifically ``StateInitDiatomAtom_Module.F90`` (3-body) and
``StateInitDiatomDiatom_Module.F90`` (4-body).


Ro-vibrational state :math:`(v, J)`
-----------------------------------

Internal states come from the pre-computed level database
(``dtb/Molecules/<species>/Bins_*/levels_cut.inp``). CoarseAIR offers four
selection strategies, chosen by the ``Bins/Nb of PESs`` and related input
blocks and implemented in ``src/BinnedMolecules/``:

- **State-to-State (StS)** — one bin per :math:`(v, J)` level. This gives
  full state-resolved output but is expensive at high temperature.
- **Vibrational Specific (VibSpec)** — bins are labelled by :math:`v` only;
  rotational sub-levels within each :math:`v` are distributed by
  their Boltzmann weight. Standard for vibrational-ladder studies.
- **Energy-based** — bins group levels within internal-energy windows. Used
  for coarse-grained models (group-specific rates).
- **Hybrid / FromFile** — user-supplied partitioning, read directly from a
  file listing levels per bin.

Within a selected bin, the specific starting level is drawn with probability
proportional to its degeneracy-weighted Boltzmann factor.


Collision energy :math:`E`
--------------------------

CoarseAIR supports two regimes:

**Fixed-energy grid** — the user specifies a list of collision energies in
the input file. Trajectories are run at each listed :math:`E`. Used for
computing excitation functions :math:`\sigma_J(E)` directly.

**Maxwell–Boltzmann thermal sampling** — energies are drawn from the
translational Maxwellian at temperature :math:`T`. Starting from

.. math::

   k(T) \;=\; 4\pi \!\int_0^\infty \sigma(w)\, f(w)\, w^3\, dw,
   \qquad
   f(w) \;=\; \left(\frac{m_e}{2\pi k_B T}\right)^{3/2}
              \exp\!\left(-\frac{m_e w^2}{2 k_B T}\right),

a change of variables to the reduced energy :math:`\hat{E} = E/(k_B T)`
gives the probability density

.. math::

   P(\hat{E}) \;=\; \hat{E}\, e^{-\hat{E}},
   \qquad
   F(\hat{E}) \;=\; 1 - e^{-\hat{E}}\,(\hat{E} + 1).

CoarseAIR draws uniform :math:`U \sim \mathcal{U}[0,1]`, inverts the CDF by
Newton–Raphson to obtain :math:`\hat{E}`, and sets
:math:`E = \hat{E}\, k_B T`. The thermal rate is then

.. math::

   k(T) \;\approx\; \sqrt{\frac{8 k_B T}{\mu\pi}}\;
                    \frac{1}{N}\sum_{p=1}^{N} \sigma(\hat{E}_p),

where :math:`\mu` is the reduced mass of the collision. The inverse-CDF
sampling is coded in ``src/Random/`` and called from the initial-state
modules.


Impact parameter :math:`b`
--------------------------

By standard kinematics, the differential cross-section in :math:`b` carries
weight :math:`2\pi b\,db`, so uniform sampling *in* :math:`b^2` over
:math:`[0, b_{\max}^2]` reproduces the correct weighting without further
Jacobians. CoarseAIR's input offers four impact-parameter models:

- ``Normal`` — uniform in :math:`b^2`, single :math:`b_{\max}`. Simplest and
  default for most dissociation / inelastic studies.
- ``Random`` — uniform :math:`b`, requires Jacobian reweighting during rate
  extraction. Mainly for diagnostic use.
- ``Stratified`` — :math:`b` is split into concentric annuli
  :math:`[b_{i-1}, b_i]` with pre-assigned trajectory counts :math:`N_i`.
  Reduces variance at small :math:`b` where reaction is most likely.
- ``FixedAngMom`` — :math:`b` is chosen to reproduce a target orbital
  angular momentum, useful for reactive-scattering studies at specified
  :math:`\ell`.

The value of :math:`b_{\max}` is system- and energy-dependent; a brief
pre-run "scattering map" (see :doc:`/examples/h3`) establishes the smallest
:math:`b` at which *no* reactive/inelastic events occur, and that value sets
:math:`b_{\max}`.


Relative-velocity decomposition
-------------------------------

Given :math:`E`, :math:`b`, and the initial separation :math:`D_{\text{init}}`
along the :math:`x` axis in the body-fixed frame, the relative velocity
magnitude and components are

.. math::

   V_{e_R} \;=\; \sqrt{\frac{2 E}{\mu}}, \qquad
   V_{e_z} \;=\; -V_{e_R}\,\frac{b}{D_{\text{init}}}, \qquad
   V_{e_x} \;=\; -V_{e_R}\,\sqrt{1 - \frac{b^2}{D_{\text{init}}^2}}.

The minus signs encode the convention that the projectile approaches the
target (which sits at the origin). :math:`V_{e_y} = 0` is imposed by
placing the collision plane to coincide with :math:`x`–:math:`z`.

The projectile's initial Cartesian position and velocity vectors are then
split between target and projectile reduced-mass fractions:

.. math::

   \mathbf{R}_T \;=\; -\frac{m_P}{m_T + m_P}\, D_{\text{init}}\, \hat{\mathbf{x}},
   \qquad
   \mathbf{R}_P \;=\; +\frac{m_T}{m_T + m_P}\, D_{\text{init}}\, \hat{\mathbf{x}}.


Orientation sampling (Euler angles)
-----------------------------------

The target diatom's orientation in the body-fixed frame is randomised by
three successive Euler rotations. Using the standard :math:`z`-:math:`y`-
:math:`z` convention :math:`(\alpha, \beta, \gamma)`:

.. math::

   \begin{pmatrix} x'\\ y'\\ z' \end{pmatrix}
   \;=\;
   R_z(\gamma)\, R_y(\beta)\, R_z(\alpha)
   \begin{pmatrix} x\\ y\\ z \end{pmatrix}.

With :math:`\alpha, \gamma` uniform on :math:`[0, 2\pi)` and
:math:`\cos\beta` uniform on :math:`[-1, 1]` (to sample the sphere
uniformly), the resulting orientation is isotropic. The angles are drawn
independently per trajectory. For 4-atom systems the same scheme is applied
to both colliding diatoms independently.


Electronic-surface index ``iPES``
---------------------------------

When multiple electronic surfaces are active (e.g. O\ :sub:`3` with 9
surfaces, CNO with 3, CO\ :sub:`2` with 5), CoarseAIR distributes
trajectories across surfaces proportionally to their electronic
degeneracies.

At initialisation, the routine ``SetPESIndx()`` (called from the collision
class) assigns each trajectory an ``iPES`` drawn from the discrete
distribution

.. math::

   P(\text{iPES} = s) \;=\; \frac{g_s}{\sum_{s'} g_{s'}},

where :math:`g_s` is the degeneracy of surface :math:`s` specified in the
input file. The per-PES trajectory counter
``Traj%iTrajPES(1:NPESs)`` tracks how many trajectories have been assigned
to each surface, so the final statistics (cross-sections, rates) are
weighted consistently.

In adiabatic CoarseAIR, ``iPES(iTraj)`` never changes after this initial
assignment — see :doc:`pes_formats` for the multi-surface machinery and the
CoarseAIR+ documentation for surface-hopping extensions.
