Quasi-Classical Trajectories
============================

CoarseAIR computes state-to-state cross-sections and rate coefficients by
the **Quasi-Classical Trajectory (QCT)** method. The name captures the
central compromise: the nuclear dynamics are integrated *classically*
on a single Born–Oppenheimer potential energy surface (PES), while the
initial and final internal states are *quantised* semi-classically. In
the regime it targets — thermal and super-thermal collisions between
light atoms on modestly reactive surfaces — QCT delivers fully
state-resolved cross-sections at a small fraction of the cost of a
proper quantum scattering calculation, and with well-understood
failure modes that can be audited a posteriori.

This chapter lays out the theory underlying every piece of the
CoarseAIR runtime, in enough detail that a reader can connect the
equations to the specific routines in ``src/Collision/``. Subsequent
theory chapters cover the algorithms that implement the ideas here
(sampling, integrator, PES evaluation, state identification, rate
extraction).


Historical context and code lineage
-----------------------------------

The modern QCT method was established by Karplus, Porter and Sharma
for H + H\ :sub:`2` in the mid-1960s and has been the workhorse
method for reactive scattering ever since. CoarseAIR's direct
numerical ancestor is **VVTC** (Vectorized Variable-Stepsize
Trajectory Code) by David W. Schwenke at NASA Ames Research Center:
Bulirsch–Stoer integration of Hamilton's equations in Jacobi
coordinates, vectorised over a batch of trajectories, with
impact-parameter sampling by stratified :math:`b^2` discs and
semi-classical binning of the final actions. CoarseAIR re-implements
VVTC's numerics in modern object-oriented Fortran 2008 and adds:

- polymorphic PES / diatomic-potential / binning / processes /
  system objects that drop in from the input file;
- a multi-surface infrastructure (:math:`N_\text{PES}` up to 9 for
  O\ :sub:`3`) with per-trajectory surface indexing;
- machine-learned potential formats (feed-forward neural networks,
  permutation-invariant NN, Gaussian processes, Bayesian NN
  ensembles) on top of the traditional analytic forms.

None of the above changes the physics: a CoarseAIR calculation on,
say, the NASA N\ :sub:`3` surface is expected to agree with a VVTC
calculation on the same surface to within Monte Carlo statistical
error. The regression test ``test/systems/N3/`` in the repo exercises
exactly this — see :doc:`/developer/regression_tests`.


The Born–Oppenheimer surface and the classical limit
----------------------------------------------------

Every CoarseAIR trajectory is propagated on a single Born–Oppenheimer
surface :math:`V(\mathbf{R})`, i.e. the electronic Schrödinger
equation has been solved (offline, by whatever method produced the
PES) at every nuclear configuration :math:`\mathbf{R}` to give a
single scalar potential. Inside the dynamics, electronic degrees of
freedom do not appear — they have been "frozen out" into
:math:`V(\mathbf{R})`.

The *classical-limit* (or WKB) approximation then treats the nuclei
as classical point masses moving on that single surface. The total
quantum-mechanical time-dependent Schrödinger equation for the
nuclear wavefunction :math:`\Psi(\mathbf{R}, t)`,

.. math::

   i\hbar\,\frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t}
   \;=\; \left[ -\sum_\alpha \frac{\hbar^2}{2m_\alpha}
                \nabla_\alpha^{2}
         \;+\; V(\mathbf{R})
        \right]\Psi,

is replaced by Hamilton's equations for a classical point-particle
trajectory :math:`\mathbf{R}(t)` with canonical momenta
:math:`\mathbf{P}(t)`. The ro-vibrational states that QCT cannot
represent explicitly are recovered by *binning* trajectory-level
observables (final arrangement, final action, final angular momentum)
onto pre-computed quantum levels — see
:doc:`arrangement_classification`.


Regime of validity
------------------

The classical limit is controlled by the de Broglie wavelength of
each nucleus relative to the length scale :math:`\ell` on which the
PES features change,

.. math::

   \lambda_\alpha \;=\; \frac{h}{p_\alpha}
                  \;=\; \frac{h}{\sqrt{2 m_\alpha E_\alpha}},
   \qquad
   \text{QCT valid when } \lambda_\alpha \ll \ell.

For atomic or ro-vibrational collisions at
:math:`T \gtrsim 1000` K, typical nuclear de Broglie wavelengths for
even the lightest atom (H, :math:`m \sim 1836\,m_e`) are
:math:`\lambda \sim 0.5\,a_0`, well below the bond-length scale
:math:`\ell \sim 1\text{--}3\,a_0` of the PES minima. For heavier
atoms (N, O, C, Ar) the ratio is an order of magnitude more
favourable.

What the classical approximation **misses**:

- **Tunnelling.** Any reactive channel whose classical barrier lies
  above the collision energy is mis-represented as closed. For
  shallow barriers :math:`\Delta V \lesssim k_B T` this is usually
  a minor correction; for high, thin barriers — notably H-atom
  transfer reactions in the cryogenic regime — it can be orders
  of magnitude.
- **Zero-point-energy (ZPE) leakage.** Classical trajectories do
  not respect the :math:`v = 0` zero-point level; vibrational
  energy can flow freely below the quantum-mechanical ground state.
  Gaussian-binning (Bonnet & Rayez) and adiabatic constraints are
  known corrections; the base CoarseAIR code uses histogram binning
  without ZPE correction (see :doc:`arrangement_classification`).
- **Quantum interference.** Different reactive pathways to the same
  final state are summed incoherently — phases are not tracked.
- **Resonances.** Sharp features in the energy dependence of a
  reactive cross-section, produced by metastable intermediate
  complexes, are either washed out or misattributed.
- **Electronic transitions between surfaces.** A single-surface
  PES is the model assumption; multi-surface runs in CoarseAIR are
  degeneracy-weighted averages over independent single-surface
  ensembles, not a coupled-channels simulation.

CoarseAIR's runtime diagnostics (energy conservation, sum rule,
detailed balance — see :doc:`rate_extraction`) detect *numerical*
failures but not these *physical* approximations; the user is
responsible for judging whether the QCT regime applies to their
chosen system and temperature.


Jacobi coordinates and reduced masses
-------------------------------------

For a 3-atom system with atoms A, B, C and masses :math:`m_A, m_B,
m_C`, CoarseAIR uses the **atom + diatom Jacobi decomposition**.
Let A and B be the two atoms of the initial target diatom and C
the projectile. Define:

.. math::

   \mathbf{r} \;=\; \mathbf{R}_B - \mathbf{R}_A, \qquad
   \mathbf{R} \;=\; \mathbf{R}_C - \frac{m_A \mathbf{R}_A + m_B \mathbf{R}_B}
                                         {m_A + m_B},

so :math:`\mathbf{r}` is the AB internuclear vector and
:math:`\mathbf{R}` is the displacement of C from the AB
centre-of-mass. The centre-of-mass motion decouples and is removed.
The associated reduced masses are

.. math::

   \mu_{AB} \;=\; \frac{m_A m_B}{m_A + m_B}, \qquad
   \mu_{AB,C} \;=\; \frac{(m_A + m_B)\,m_C}{m_A + m_B + m_C},

and the total kinetic energy in these coordinates reads

.. math::

   T \;=\; \frac{\|\dot{\mathbf{r}}\|^{2}}{2}\,\mu_{AB}
       \;+\; \frac{\|\dot{\mathbf{R}}\|^{2}}{2}\,\mu_{AB,C}.

The corresponding conjugate momenta are
:math:`\mathbf{p} = \mu_{AB}\dot{\mathbf{r}}` and
:math:`\mathbf{P} = \mu_{AB,C}\dot{\mathbf{R}}`, giving six position
and six momentum components — hence ``NEqtTot = 12`` for a 3-atom
system. CoarseAIR packages these components into the phase-space
vector ``PaQ`` (momenta first, coordinates second).

For a 4-atom system A, B, C, D the analogous **diatom + diatom
Jacobi decomposition** uses:

.. math::

   \mathbf{r}_1 \;=\; \mathbf{R}_B - \mathbf{R}_A,
   \qquad
   \mathbf{r}_2 \;=\; \mathbf{R}_D - \mathbf{R}_C,
   \qquad
   \mathbf{R}_{12} \;=\; \mathbf{R}_{CD}^{CM} - \mathbf{R}_{AB}^{CM},

with three reduced masses (:math:`\mu_{AB}`, :math:`\mu_{CD}`,
:math:`\mu_{AB,CD}`) and ``NEqtTot = 18`` phase-space components.
The transformation logic lives in
``src/Collision/Transformation_Class.F90``; the mass factors
:math:`\mu` enter ``mMiMn`` inside each PES object during
initialisation, so the integrator sees them as plain constants
during the hot path.


Pair numbering convention
-------------------------

A key convention carried throughout the code and the output files:

**3-atom systems** have three atoms A, B, C with three atom pairs:

.. math::

   \text{pair 1} = (A, B), \qquad
   \text{pair 2} = (A, C), \qquad
   \text{pair 3} = (B, C).

**4-atom systems** have four atoms A, B, C, D and six atom pairs
(see the ``Notation`` figure in the legacy User's Manual):

.. math::

   \begin{array}{llll}
     1 = (A, B), & 2 = (A, C), & 3 = (A, D), & \\
     4 = (B, C), & 5 = (B, D), & 6 = (C, D). &
   \end{array}

The convention is used by the ``AtomsPair`` module
(``src/AtomsPairs/``), by the final-state classifier when it
reports ``arr_f``, and — through the ``16 m + n`` encoding — by
the trajectory-output file (:doc:`/user/postprocessing`).


Hamiltonian and canonical equations of motion
---------------------------------------------

Starting from the Lagrangian
:math:`L = T - V` in the Jacobi coordinates above, the Legendre
transform gives the Hamiltonian

.. math::

   H(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{Q})
   \;=\; \sum_{k = 1}^{N_{\text{eq}}}
         \frac{p_k^{2}}{2\, m_k^{\text{eff}}}
   \;+\; V(\mathbf{Q}),

where :math:`\{m_k^{\text{eff}}\}` are the Jacobi reduced masses
(``mMiMn`` on the PES object) and :math:`V(\mathbf{Q})` is the PES
value on the trajectory's assigned electronic surface. Hamilton's
equations

.. math::

   \frac{dQ_k}{dt}
     \;=\;  \frac{\partial H}{\partial p_k}
     \;=\;  \frac{p_k}{m_k^{\text{eff}}},
   \qquad
   \frac{dp_k}{dt}
     \;=\; -\frac{\partial H}{\partial Q_k}
     \;=\; -\frac{\partial V}{\partial Q_k}

are the system integrated by the Bulirsch–Stoer method in
:doc:`integrators`. The right-hand side is assembled in
``HamiltonODESystem`` (``src/Collision/ODE/ODE_System.F90``), which
delegates the PES gradient to the PES object via
``Collision%Compute_PES``:

.. code-block:: fortran

   ! simplified: the per-trajectory PES dispatch
   call PESsContainer( iPES(iTraj) )%PES% &
        Compute( Q(:,iTraj), V(iTraj), dVdQ(:,iTraj), Rpi(:,iTraj) )

The Hamiltonian :math:`H` is a conserved quantity of the exact flow;
numerically it drifts by a small amount each step, and the
trajectory-level :math:`\Delta H = H_{\text{final}} - H_{\text{initial}}`
is monitored as the primary integration-quality diagnostic (see
:doc:`integrators`).


Semi-classical quantisation at the boundaries
---------------------------------------------

QCT uses quantum-mechanical state assignments only at the
trajectory endpoints — never during the propagation. At both the
initial and final times, CoarseAIR applies a semi-classical
correspondence:

**Vibrational action.** For a bound diatom with reduced mass
:math:`\mu` and internal energy :math:`E_\text{int}`, the
Bohr–Sommerfeld quantisation condition is

.. math::

   \oint p(R)\,dR
   \;=\; 2 \int_{R_\text{in}}^{R_\text{out}}
         \sqrt{2\,\mu\,[E_\text{int} - V_{\text{eff}}(R;J)]}\, dR
   \;=\; 2\pi\hbar\,\left(v + \tfrac{1}{2}\right),

with :math:`R_\text{in}`, :math:`R_\text{out}` the classical
turning points. Inverting for :math:`v` gives the vibrational
quantum number used in the output. This is the same integral that
the level generator evaluates at each eigenvalue of the radial
Schrödinger equation — see :doc:`level_generation`.

**Rotational action.** The Langer-corrected mapping between the
classical squared angular momentum and the rotational quantum
number is

.. math::

   \|\mathbf{L}\|^{2} \;=\; \left(J + \tfrac{1}{2}\right)^{2}\hbar^{2},
   \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
   J + \tfrac{1}{2} \;=\; \frac{\|\mathbf{L}\|}{\hbar}.

CoarseAIR computes :math:`\mathbf{L}` from the final-state atomic
positions and velocities in the product diatom's CM frame, then
solves for :math:`J`. Histogram binning rounds :math:`J` (and
:math:`v`) to the nearest integer before they are written to the
trajectory output; the unrounded float values are retained so the
user can post-process with a different binning strategy (Gaussian,
QSS) if desired.

Both quantum numbers are then mapped onto the pre-computed level
table for the product diatom — i.e., onto a specific
:math:`(v, J)` index (state-to-state mode) or group index
(coarse-grained mode) — using the binning strategy specified in
``CoarseAIR.inp``. See :doc:`sampling` for the binning framework
and :doc:`arrangement_classification` for the full final-state
pipeline.


Per-trajectory runtime pipeline
-------------------------------

Putting the pieces together, the life of one trajectory is:

1. **Initial-state sampling** — draw :math:`(v_i, J_i)`, collision
   energy :math:`E`, impact parameter :math:`b`, Euler
   orientation angles, and PES index ``iPES`` from the
   distributions described in :doc:`sampling`.
2. **Build** ``PaQ`` in the body-fixed frame at initial
   separation :math:`D_\text{init}` along :math:`x`.
3. **Propagate** by Bulirsch–Stoer until ``tmax`` or product
   separation ``RpiMax`` (:doc:`integrators`).
4. **Final-state identification** — sort inter-atomic distances,
   compute the effective potential, classify as bound /
   quasi-bound / dissociated, compute action integrals, assign
   :math:`(v', J')` and arrangement code
   (:doc:`arrangement_classification`).
5. **Emit** one row of ``trajectories.csv`` with
   :math:`(i_\text{traj}, \text{iPES}, b_{\max}, b,
   J_i, v_i, \text{arr}_i, J_f, v_f, \text{arr}_f)`.

The cross-section and rate-coefficient estimators that consume this
output are derived in :doc:`rate_extraction`.


What CoarseAIR does *not* do in the adiabatic pipeline
------------------------------------------------------

A short consolidated list for the record (all are design choices,
not bugs):

- **No dynamical electronic transitions.** Each trajectory stays on
  the surface ``iPES`` was initialised to. Multi-surface runs are
  therefore a *degeneracy-weighted average* over independent
  single-surface ensembles, not a coupled-channels simulation.
- **No Gaussian-binning ZPE correction** in the base code. Final
  :math:`v, J` are assigned by histogram binning to the nearest
  pre-computed level. Post-processing tools can apply Gaussian
  binning or QSS-style weighting if desired.
- **No quantum interference** between reactive pathways.
- **No backward-propagation cross-check** in production runs. A
  ``Backward Integrating? = yes`` option exists for diagnostics
  but is not the normal mode.
- **No relativistic or spin-orbit corrections** beyond what is
  already baked into the PES. Different spin-orbit components of
  the same electronic manifold are separate PES instances in the
  input (e.g. the three CNO electronic surfaces).

These limits set the boundary of the QCT regime; whenever a user's
application approaches one of them, the run should be cross-checked
against an independent quantum or semi-classical method.
