Quasi-Classical Trajectories
============================

CoarseAIR computes state-to-state cross-sections and rate coefficients using
the **Quasi-Classical Trajectory (QCT)** method: the nuclear dynamics are
integrated classically on a single Born–Oppenheimer potential energy surface
(PES), while the initial and final internal states are *quantised*
semi-classically. This chapter sets out the theoretical framework CoarseAIR
implements, referencing the specific data structures and code paths involved.


Classical-limit approximation
-----------------------------

For collisions at thermal or super-thermal energies typical of re-entry and
high-enthalpy flows, nuclear de Broglie wavelengths are much smaller than the
length scales of the PES features probed. In that regime the nuclei can be
treated as classical point masses moving on a single adiabatic PES — the QCT
approximation. CoarseAIR solves Hamilton's equations for each trajectory and
then bins the resulting final states onto quantised ro-vibrational levels.

The approximation breaks down where quantum effects dominate — tunnelling,
zero-point leakage, resonances, interference between reactive pathways, and
coupled electronic states — but it is well-established as a cost-effective,
fully state-resolved method for the temperature range targeted here
(:math:`\sim\!1\,000` K to :math:`\sim\!20\,000` K). Nonadiabatic effects are
addressed separately in the CoarseAIR+ fork via fewest-switches surface
hopping.


Coordinates and pair numbering
------------------------------

**3-atom systems** have three atoms A, B, C, with three atom pairs:

.. math::

   \text{pair 1} = (A, B), \qquad \text{pair 2} = (A, C), \qquad
   \text{pair 3} = (B, C).

**4-atom systems** have four atoms A, B, C, D, and six atom pairs:

.. math::

   1 = (A, B), \quad 2 = (A, C), \quad 3 = (A, D),
   \quad 4 = (B, C), \quad 5 = (B, D), \quad 6 = (C, D).

This is the convention used throughout the input files, the ``AtomsPair``
module (``src/AtomsPairs/``), and the processes classification in
``src/Processes/``.

CoarseAIR stores each trajectory in a **phase-space vector** ``PaQ`` of length
``NEqtTot`` packed as momenta first, coordinates second:

- 3-atom system: ``NEqtVar = 6``, ``NEqtTot = 12``
  (6 momentum components :math:`p_i` + 6 Jacobi-like coordinates :math:`Q_i`).
- 4-atom system: ``NEqtVar = 9``, ``NEqtTot = 18``.

Centre-of-mass motion has been removed, and the internal coordinates are
expressed in a body-fixed frame where the target molecule sits at the origin
and the projectile enters along the :math:`z` axis at an offset :math:`b`
(the impact parameter). See :doc:`sampling` for the transformation from
asymptotic collision coordinates to the full ``PaQ`` vector.


Hamiltonian
-----------

Each trajectory obeys

.. math::

   H(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{Q}) \;=\;
   \sum_{k} \frac{p_k^2}{2\,m_k^{\text{eff}}}
   \;+\; V\!\left( \mathbf{Q} \right),

where the effective masses :math:`m_k^{\text{eff}}` (stored as ``mMiMn`` on
the PES object) come from the Jacobi-coordinate transformation and
:math:`V(\mathbf{Q})` is the PES evaluation on the surface the trajectory is
currently assigned to. Hamilton's equations

.. math::

   \frac{dQ_k}{dt} \;=\; \frac{\partial H}{\partial p_k},
   \qquad
   \frac{dp_k}{dt} \;=\; -\frac{\partial H}{\partial Q_k}

are integrated by the Bulirsch–Stoer scheme described in :doc:`integrators`.
The force term :math:`\partial V / \partial Q_k` is delivered by the PES
module (``src/Collision/ODE/ODE_System.F90`` →
``Collision%Compute_PES(iPES, Q, dVdQ, V, Rpi)``), which dispatches to the
appropriate PES object:

.. code-block:: fortran

   ! Per-trajectory PES dispatch (simplified)
   call PESsContainer( iPES(iTraj) )%PES%Compute( Q, dVdQ, V, Rpi )

The scalar ``iPES(iTraj)`` stores the electronic surface index that
trajectory ``iTraj`` runs on. In adiabatic CoarseAIR this index is fixed at
initialisation; in CoarseAIR+ it may change mid-trajectory via a surface-hop
event.


Per-trajectory pipeline
-----------------------

For each trajectory the code executes the following sequence
(``src/Collision/Integrator_Class.F90``):

1. **Initial-state sampling.** Select initial ro-vibrational quantum numbers
   :math:`(v, J)`, impact parameter :math:`b`, collision energy :math:`E`,
   orientation angles, and PES index. See :doc:`sampling`.
2. **Build the initial** ``PaQ`` **vector** in the body-fixed frame, with
   projectile at separation :math:`D_{\text{init}}` along :math:`x`.
3. **Propagate** ``PaQ`` forward in time via Bulirsch–Stoer until one of two
   convergence criteria trips:

   - :math:`t \geq t_{\max}` (hard time limit), or
   - some inter-atomic distance exceeds ``RpiMax`` (products fully
     separated).

4. **Identify the final arrangement** by sorting inter-atomic distances and
   running the effective-potential classifier. See
   :doc:`arrangement_classification`.
5. **Quantise the final state** semi-classically:

   .. math::

      v' + \tfrac{1}{2}
      \;=\; \frac{1}{\pi\hbar}
      \oint p\,dq,
      \qquad
      J'(J'+1)\hbar^2 \;\leftarrow\; \|\mathbf{L}\|^{2}.

   The action integral :math:`\oint p\,dq` is evaluated by the diatomic
   potential object (``DiatomicPotential%ActionIntegral``); the angular
   momentum :math:`\mathbf{L}` is computed from the final atomic velocities
   in the product diatom's CM frame.

The outputs per trajectory are: the process type (elastic, inelastic,
exchange, dissociation), the final :math:`(v', J')`, the PES index
``iPES``, the impact parameter :math:`b`, and the final Hamiltonian for
energy-conservation audit.


What CoarseAIR does *not* do (at this level)
--------------------------------------------

- **No nonadiabatic transitions** in the main adiabatic branch. Each
  trajectory stays on the surface assigned at initialisation. The full
  multi-surface machinery is exercised only for degeneracy-weighted
  branching, not for dynamical hops — see :doc:`pes_formats` and the
  CoarseAIR+ documentation.
- **No zero-point leakage control.** CoarseAIR uses standard histogram
  binning of final actions; no Gaussian-binning or zero-point-energy
  correction is applied in the base workflow.
- **No quantum interference.** Reactive branches are counted independently;
  no phase information is tracked.
