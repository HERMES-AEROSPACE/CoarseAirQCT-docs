N\ :sub:`2`\ O System
=====================

The **N₂O** system is a three-body arrangement with two nitrogens and
one oxygen. The two N atoms are indistinguishable. Input files live
under ``input/N2O/``.

Arrangement
-----------

- Atoms: **N, N, O**. The two N atoms are chemically identical.
- Collision entries: N\ :sub:`2` + O or NO + N.

Kinetic processes
-----------------

- **Inelastic scattering** on the incoming arrangement:
  N\ :sub:`2`\ (v, J) + O, or NO(v, J) + N.
- **Reactive exchange** between the two distinct arrangements:

  .. math::

     \mathrm{N}_{2} + \mathrm{O} \;\leftrightarrow\;
     \mathrm{NO} + \mathrm{N}.

  This is the Zel'dovich-family reaction and is a key channel for NO
  formation in high-temperature air.
- **Dissociation** — full atomisation :math:`\to` N + N + O.

The two "NO + N" pathways (NO\ :sub:`a` + N\ :sub:`b` and NO\ :sub:`b`
+ N\ :sub:`a`) are summed in indistinguishable mode.

Multi-surface structure
-----------------------

Low-lying N\ :sub:`2`\ O surfaces of relevance: :math:`{}^{1}A'`
(``Basel_13A1``), :math:`{}^{1}A''` (``Basel_13A2``). Multi-surface
runs are degeneracy-weighted.

PES models
----------

Available: ``Basel`` (multiple surface labels), ``UMN``.

Diatomic partners: ``N2_UMN_ForN2O2``, ``NO_UMN``, ``O2_UMN_ForN2O2``.

Typical use
-----------

N\ :sub:`2` + O :math:`\to` NO + N drives the formation of NO in the
shock layer of hypersonic re-entry and is one of the canonical "extended
Zel'dovich" reactions in air chemistry.
