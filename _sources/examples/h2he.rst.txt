H\ :sub:`2` + He System
=======================

The **H₂ + He** system is a three-body, **non-reactive** arrangement used
to study rotational and vibrational relaxation of molecular hydrogen by
helium. Input files live under ``input/H2He/``.

Arrangement
-----------

- Atoms: **H, H, He**. The two H atoms are identical; He is chemically
  inert.
- Collision entry: H\ :sub:`2` + He.
- Pair numbering: pair 1 = H-H (reactive bond), pairs 2 and 3 =
  H-He (dummy pair potentials).

Kinetic processes
-----------------

- **Rotational relaxation** — :math:`\mathrm{H}_{2}(J) + \mathrm{He}
  \to \mathrm{H}_{2}(J') + \mathrm{He}` at the same :math:`v`.
- **Vibrational relaxation** — :math:`\mathrm{H}_{2}(v, J) + \mathrm{He}
  \to \mathrm{H}_{2}(v', J') + \mathrm{He}`.
- No exchange channel (He cannot form a stable bond to H on the PES
  considered).
- Dissociation :math:`\mathrm{H}_{2}(v, J) + \mathrm{He} \to \mathrm{H}
  + \mathrm{H} + \mathrm{He}` accessible at very high energy.

Because the two H atoms are identical, the two "equivalent" final-state
pathways are aggregated in indistinguishable mode.

PES models
----------

Available: ``BMPmod`` (a modified Boothroyd–Martin–Peterson PES).

Diatomic partners: ``H2_BMKP`` (H\ :sub:`2`), ``HHe_Dummy`` (inert).

Typical use
-----------

H\ :sub:`2` + He is a benchmark system for astrophysical and
laser-cooling applications, where rotational populations of H\
:sub:`2` are probed in cold, He-rich environments. The small number
of active degrees of freedom makes it an excellent sanity check for the
CoarseAIR integration pipeline.
