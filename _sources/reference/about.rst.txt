About CoarseAIR
===============

CoarseAIR is a Fortran 2008 object-oriented code for state-to-state
and coarse-grained quasi-classical trajectory (QCT) simulations of
atomic and molecular collisions on *ab initio* potential energy
surfaces. It was originally developed to produce the state-resolved
rate-coefficient databases needed for non-equilibrium aerothermodynamic
simulations of hypersonic re-entry.

Related codes
-------------

CoarseAIR is one of three production codes shipped by the HERMES
group; together they form a multiscale pipeline from ab-initio
chemistry to hypersonic CFD:

- **CoarseAIR** — produces state-resolved rate coefficients on
  ab-initio PES via QCT.
- **dmsAIR** — direct molecular simulation (DSMC of state-resolved
  kinetics) that consumes CoarseAIR rate databases.
  https://hermes-aerospace.github.io/dmsAIR-docs/
- **PLATO** — multi-temperature / state-to-state CFD solver that
  consumes the same chemistry databases.

A companion post-processing Python tool, **PyCoarseAIR**
(https://github.com/simoneventuri/PyCoarseAIR), packages CoarseAIR
output into the HDF5 format the downstream codes expect.


CoarseAIR+ (surface-hopping extension)
--------------------------------------

A separate fork of CoarseAIR — **CoarseAIR+** — adds fewest-switches
surface hopping (FSSH) for explicit electronic non-adiabatic
transitions. The initial target system is ozone (O\ :sub:`3`) with
its nine electronic surfaces; subsequent systems (CO\ :sub:`2`,
NO\ :sub:`2`, CNO) follow. CoarseAIR+ shares the QCT machinery
documented on this site; the surface-hopping layer is documented in a
separate companion site.


Citation guidance
-----------------

If you use CoarseAIR, please cite the references most relevant to your
work — see :doc:`bibliography` for the full list. The canonical entry
points are:

- **S. Venturi**, *Machine Learning and Uncertainty Quantification
  Framework for Predictive Ab Initio Hypersonics*, Ph.D. Thesis,
  University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (2021).
- **S. Venturi, R. L. Jaffe, M. Panesi**, *Bayesian Machine Learning
  Approach to the Quantification of Uncertainties on Ab Initio
  Potential Energy Surfaces*, J. Phys. Chem. A **124** (25),
  5129–5146 (2020).
- **S. Venturi, M. P. Sharma, B. Lopez, M. Panesi**,
  *Data-Inspired and Physics-Driven Model Reduction for Dissociation:
  Application to the O\ :sub:`2` + O System*, J. Phys. Chem. A
  **124** (41), 8359–8372 (2020).

If you use a specific PES (e.g. the UMN O\ :sub:`3` surface, the
NASA N\ :sub:`4` surface), please also cite the original PES paper —
see :doc:`bibliography`.


Acknowledgements
----------------

The CoarseAIR code directly builds on the numerical methods and
design choices of David Schwenke's VVTC at NASA Ames, and incorporates
PES implementations from the groups of Donald Truhlar (University of
Minnesota), Markus Meuwly (Basel), Hua Guo (University of New Mexico),
Richard Jaffe (NASA Ames), and António Varandas (Aveiro), among
others.

The documentation site you are reading is maintained by Clement
Civrais in the HERMES Aerospace group, with sources in
``docs/online-documentation/``.
