Bibliography
============

Canonical references for the CoarseAIR methodology and for the most
commonly used potential energy surfaces. Use these when citing
CoarseAIR or any surface it ships.


CoarseAIR foundations
---------------------

- S. Venturi,
  *Machine Learning and Uncertainty Quantification Framework for
  Predictive Ab Initio Hypersonics*, Ph.D. thesis, University of
  Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (2021).
- S. Venturi, R. L. Jaffe, M. Panesi,
  *Bayesian Machine Learning Approach to the Quantification of
  Uncertainties on Ab Initio Potential Energy Surfaces*,
  J. Phys. Chem. A **124** (25), 5129–5146 (2020).
  `doi:10.1021/acs.jpca.0c02395
  <https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.jpca.0c02395>`_
- S. Venturi, M. P. Sharma, B. Lopez, M. Panesi,
  *Data-Inspired and Physics-Driven Model Reduction for Dissociation:
  Application to the O*\ :sub:`2` *+ O System*,
  J. Phys. Chem. A **124** (41), 8359–8372 (2020).
  `doi:10.1021/acs.jpca.0c04516
  <https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.jpca.0c04516>`_
- R. L. Jaffe, D. W. Schwenke, M. Panesi,
  *First Principles Calculation of Heavy Particle Rate Coefficients*,
  Chapter 2 in *Hypersonic Nonequilibrium Flows: Fundamentals and
  Recent Advances* (AIAA, 2014).


Numerical methods
-----------------

- A. W. Sandvik,
  *Numerical Solutions of the Schrödinger Equation*, Boston
  University lecture notes (2016). Reference for the Numerov method
  used in level generation.
- B. R. Johnson,
  *The renormalized Numerov method applied to calculating bound
  states of the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation*,
  J. Chem. Phys. **69**, 4678 (1978).
  `doi:10.1063/1.436421 <https://doi.org/10.1063/1.436421>`_
  — coupled-channel generalisation of the Numerov solver.
- W. H. Press, S. A. Teukolsky, W. T. Vetterling, B. P. Flannery,
  *Numerical Recipes: The Art of Scientific Computing* (3rd ed.,
  Cambridge University Press, 2007). Bulirsch–Stoer integrator and
  modified-midpoint method; see :doc:`/theory/integrators`.
- L. Bonnet, J.-C. Rayez,
  *Quasiclassical trajectory method for molecular scattering
  processes*, Chem. Phys. Lett. **277**, 183–190 (1997).
  `doi:10.1016/S0009-2614(97)00881-6
  <https://doi.org/10.1016/S0009-2614(97)00881-6>`_
  — Gaussian-binning correction for final-state quantisation. Not
  yet implemented in CoarseAIR, tracked on
  :doc:`/developer/roadmap`.


Potential energy surfaces
-------------------------

When citing CoarseAIR runs, please also cite the original PES paper
for the surface actually used. The list below pairs each CoarseAIR
PES model name with the paper that introduced it. Where we have a
confirmed DOI it is linked inline; where not, please file a pull
request / memo to ``docs/online-documentation/reference/bibliography.rst``
with the missing reference.


CNO system
~~~~~~~~~~

The three low-lying electronic surfaces used by the CoarseAIR CNO
system are the *accurate DMBE* potential energy surfaces of the
Varandas / UCI-adjacent group:

- **CNO** :math:`{}^{2}A'` **(**\ ``CNO_UCI_2A1``\ **)** —
  Mota, Caridade, Varandas,
  *Accurate DMBE potential energy surface for CNO*\ (:math:`{}^{2}A'`\ ),
  J. Phys. Chem. A **122** (23), 5173–5180 (2018).
  `doi:10.1021/acs.jpca.8b01881
  <https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.jpca.8b01881>`_
- **CNO** :math:`{}^{2}A''` **(**\ ``CNO_UCI_2A2``\ **)** —
  Mota, Varandas and co-workers,
  *Accurate DMBE potential energy surface for CNO*\ (:math:`{}^{2}A''`\ ),
  J. Chem. Phys. **154**, 034303 (2021).
  `doi:10.1063/5.0039691
  <https://pubs.aip.org/aip/jcp/article/154/3/034303/199924/Accurate-DMBE-potential-energy-surface-for-CNO-2A>`_
- **CNO** :math:`{}^{4}A''` **(**\ ``CNO_UCI_4A2``\ **)** —
  Varandas and co-workers,
  *High-accuracy DMBE potential energy surface for*
  :math:`{}^{4}A''` *CNO*,
  J. Chem. Phys. **159**, 214307 (2023).
  `doi:10.1063/5.0174547
  <https://pubs.aip.org/aip/jcp/article/159/21/214307/2926417/High-accuracy-DMBE-potential-energy-surface-for>`_

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

- **H**\ :sub:`3` **BKMP2 (**\ ``H3_BH``\ **)** — A. I. Boothroyd,

- **H**\ :sub:`4` **BMKP (**\ ``H4_BMKP``\ **)** —

Nitrogen systems
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

- **N**\ :sub:`3` **NASA (**\ ``N3_NASA``\ **)** —
  M. Panesi, R. L. Jaffe, D. W. Schwenke, T. E. Magin,
  *Rovibrational internal energy transfer and dissociation of
  N*\ :sub:`2`\  (:math:`^{1}\Sigma^{+}_{g}`\ ) + *N*\ (:math:`^{4}S`\ )
  *system in hypersonic flows*, J. Chem. Phys. **138**, 044312 (2013).
  `doi:10.1063/1.4774412 <https://doi.org/10.1063/1.4774412>`_
- **N**\ :sub:`3` **UMN / UMN\_MBPIPMEG** —
  Z. Varga, Y. Paukku, D. G. Truhlar and co-workers, N\ :sub:`2`\ –N
  triplet surfaces using Mixed Exponential Gaussian and
  permutation-invariant polynomial forms (University of Minnesota).
- **N**\ :sub:`4` **NASA / UMN / UMN\_PIPNN** — UMN neural-network
  training of the four-body N\ :sub:`2`\ –N\ :sub:`2` surface.
- **N**\ :sub:`2`\ **O Basel / UMN** — Meuwly group (Basel) and
  Truhlar group (UMN) three-body surfaces for the Zel'dovich
  reaction.


Oxygen systems
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

- **O**\ :sub:`3` **UMN — 9 electronic surfaces** —
  Z. Varga, Y. Paukku, D. G. Truhlar,
  *Potential energy surfaces for O + O*\ :sub:`2` *collisions*,
  series of papers (2017–2019) covering the
  1\ :sup:`1`\ A', 1\ :sup:`1`\ A'', 3A', 3A'', 5A', 5A'',
  2\ :sup:`1`\ A', 2\ :sup:`3`\ A', 2\ :sup:`5`\ A' manifold.
- **O**\ :sub:`3` **NN / BNN / GP** — neural-network, Bayesian-NN,
  and Gaussian-process regressions of the UMN ab-initio data,
  Venturi, Jaffe, Panesi (HERMES group, 2020 — see CoarseAIR
  foundations above).
- **O**\ :sub:`4` **UMN** — Paukku, Yang, Varga, Truhlar four-body
  O\ :sub:`4` surface (UMN).


Carbon-containing systems
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

- **CO**\ :sub:`2` **NASA / Basel** — Schwenke and Jaffe (NASA Ames)
  and Meuwly (Basel) three-body surfaces for C + O\ :sub:`2` / O +
  CO kinetics.
- **CO + Ar NASA** — Schwenke and Jaffe non-reactive CO–Ar surface.
- **CHN UIUC / GUO** — Illinois and Guo (UNM) surfaces for C + HN /
  H + CN / N + CH reactive kinetics.
- **NO**\ :sub:`2` **UMN / Basel** — UMN and Meuwly three-body
  surfaces for NO\ :sub:`2` kinetics, with 2\ :sup:`2,4`\ A',
  2\ :sup:`2,4`\ A'' manifold.

Recommended reading
-------------------

- M. Karplus, R. N. Porter, R. D. Sharma,
  *Exchange Reactions with Activation Energy. I. Simple Barrier
  Potential for (H, H*\ :sub:`2`\ *)*,
  J. Chem. Phys. **43**, 3259 (1965).
  `doi:10.1063/1.1697301 <https://doi.org/10.1063/1.1697301>`_
  — the founding paper of the QCT method.
- D. G. Truhlar, J. T. Muckerman,
  *Reactive Scattering Cross Sections: Quasiclassical and
  Semiclassical Methods*, in *Atom–Molecule Collision Theory*,
  R. B. Bernstein (Ed.), Plenum (1979) — standard textbook treatment.
- R. E. Langer,
  *On the Connection Formulas and the Solutions of the Wave
  Equation*, Phys. Rev. **51**, 669 (1937) — origin of the
  :math:`\ell(\ell+1) \to (\ell + \tfrac{1}{2})^{2}` correction used
  throughout CoarseAIR.
