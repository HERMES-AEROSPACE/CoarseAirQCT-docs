Rate & Cross-Section Extraction

After trajectory integration and final-state classification, CoarseAIR converts the raw “which-process-happened” counters into statistically well-defined cross-sections and rate coefficients. This chapter derives the estimators the code uses, their variance, and the thermal-averaging procedure that produces the final \(k(T)\).

The implementation is in src/Processes/ (per-process accumulators) and in the TrajectoriesStats / PostTrajectories application stages.

Cross-section estimator

Consider a specific initial state \((v, J)\) on a specific PES at a fixed collision energy \(E\). Suppose we ran \(N_i\) trajectories at each impact-parameter bin \(i\) (with annulus \([b_{i-1}, b_i]\)), and \(N_{iJ}\) of them produced the target process \(J\) — an inelastic transition to a specific \((v', J')\), an exchange channel, a dissociation outcome, etc.

Within bin \(i\) the probability of process \(J\) is estimated by the Bernoulli proportion

\[\hat{P}_{iJ} \;=\; \frac{N_{iJ}}{N_i}.\]

Writing the cross-section as an integral over the impact-parameter plane,

\[\sigma_J \;=\; \int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\int_0^\infty \tilde{P}_J(b, \theta)\, b\, db\, d\theta \;\approx\; \sum_i A_{\text{ring},i}\, \hat{P}_{iJ},\]

with annulus area \(A_{\text{ring},i} = \pi (b_i^2 - b_{i-1}^2)\). The CoarseAIR estimator is therefore

\[\boxed{\; \hat{\sigma}_J \;=\; \sum_{i} \pi \left(b_i^2 - b_{i-1}^2\right) \frac{N_{iJ}}{N_i} \;}\]

with \(b_0 = 0\). The summation runs over all impact-parameter bins up to \(b_{\max}\) (see Sampling).

Variance and uncertainty

Because each trajectory in bin \(i\) is an independent Bernoulli trial with success probability \(P_{iJ}\), the count \(N_{iJ}\) has binomial variance \(N_i\, P_{iJ}\,(1 - P_{iJ})\). Propagating to the cross-section estimator,

\[\operatorname{Var}(\hat{\sigma}_J) \;=\; \sum_i A_{\text{ring},i}^{2}\, \frac{\hat{P}_{iJ}(1 - \hat{P}_{iJ})}{N_i},\]

so the standard deviation is

\[\boxed{\; \operatorname{SD}(\hat{\sigma}_J) \;=\; \sqrt{\sum_i A_{\text{ring},i}^{2}\, \frac{\hat{P}_{iJ}\,(1 - \hat{P}_{iJ})}{N_i}} \;}\]

The relative statistical error of a rare event scales as \(1/\sqrt{N\, P_J}\), so low-probability channels (e.g. dissociation at low \(T\)) require many trajectories to reach tolerable error bars. This is a primary reason for stratified impact-parameter sampling (see Sampling) — concentrating trajectories at small \(b\), where reaction is more likely, reduces the variance at fixed cost.

For two complementary processes \(J\) and \(J'\) (e.g. “exchange” versus “everything else”), \(N_{iJ} + N_{iJ'} = N_i\) gives the simplified form

\[\operatorname{SD}(\hat{\sigma}_J) \;=\; \sqrt{\sum_i \frac{\hat{\sigma}_{iJ}\, \hat{\sigma}_{iJ'}}{N_i}},\]

used as a cross-check inside the code.

Thermal rate coefficient

The thermal rate constant at temperature \(T\) is the Maxwell–Boltzmann average

\[k(T) \;=\; \left\langle \sigma\, w \right\rangle \;=\; 4\pi \int_0^\infty \sigma(w)\, f(w)\, w^3\, dw, \qquad f(w) \;=\; \left(\frac{m_e}{2\pi k_B T}\right)^{3/2} \exp\!\left(-\frac{m_e w^2}{2 k_B T}\right),\]

where \(m_e\) is the reduced mass of the collision.

If the user runs trajectories at a fixed grid of collision energies, CoarseAIR performs the integral numerically (Gauss-Legendre quadrature, Tools/GaussQuadrature) on \(E = \tfrac{1}{2} m_e w^2\).

If the user runs trajectories in thermal mode, collision energies are drawn directly from the Maxwellian via inverse-CDF sampling (see Sampling). Substituting \(E = k_B T\, \hat{E}\), the rate collapses to a simple Monte Carlo average:

\[\boxed{\; k(T) \;\approx\; \sqrt{\frac{8 k_B T}{\mu \pi}}\; \frac{1}{N}\sum_{p=1}^{N} \sigma\!\left(\hat{E}_p\right) \;}\]

where \(N\) is the total number of trajectories at that temperature. No quadrature is needed — the energy distribution is built into the sampling itself.

This is the formula used by PostTrajectories to produce the final \(k(T)\) tables from a thermal run.

State-to-state, grouped, and Arrhenius outputs

CoarseAIR produces three levels of coarse-grained output; see Post-processing for the file layout.

  • State-to-state (StS). One rate per pair of levels: \(k_{(v, J) \to (v', J')}(T)\). Largest output; suitable for direct use in state-to-state master-equation solvers (About CoarseAIR → dmsAIR).

  • Grouped rates. Rates are summed into bins defined by the binning strategy used at input (vibrational, energy-based, or user-provided groups). Typically \(10^3\)–\(10^4\) rates per temperature.

  • Arrhenius fits. Effective three-parameter \(A, n, E_a\) fits in the form

    \[k(T) \;=\; A\, T^{n}\, \exp(-E_a / k_B T),\]

    are generated per process and stored in the Process%ArrCoeffs(3) field. Useful for multiscale solvers where the full temperature dependence is needed in closed form.

Error bars propagate consistently across all three aggregations — the binomial variance from the cross-section stage is carried through grouping and summation by standard Gaussian error propagation.

Complementarity and mass-balance checks

CoarseAIR exploits two self-consistency checks that users should always inspect before trusting a run:

  1. Sum rule across outcomes — for every starting level, the cross-sections for all outgoing channels (elastic + inelastic + exchange + dissociation) should sum to \(\pi b_{\max}^2\) within statistical error. Deviations flag either a too-small \(b_{\max}\) (reactive cross-section is cut off) or a classification bug.

  2. Detailed balance — for a pair of states \((v, J)\) and \((v', J')\) at the same temperature,

    \[g_{vJ}\, k_{vJ \to v'J'}(T)\, \exp\!\left(-E_{vJ}/k_B T\right) \;=\; g_{v'J'}\, k_{v'J' \to vJ}(T)\, \exp\!\left(-E_{v'J'}/k_B T\right),\]

    where \(g\) are the rotational degeneracies. Violations are expected within the QCT statistical error; persistent systematic violations (>2–3 σ) point at zero-point-energy artefacts or classification errors.

Both checks are available as diagnostics in the post-processing Python and MATLAB tooling.
