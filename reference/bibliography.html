Bibliography

Canonical references for the CoarseAIR methodology and for the most commonly used potential energy surfaces. Use these when citing CoarseAIR or any surface it ships.

CoarseAIR foundations

  • S. Venturi, Machine Learning and Uncertainty Quantification Framework for Predictive Ab Initio Hypersonics, Ph.D. thesis, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (2021).

  • S. Venturi, R. L. Jaffe, M. Panesi, Bayesian Machine Learning Approach to the Quantification of Uncertainties on Ab Initio Potential Energy Surfaces, J. Phys. Chem. A 124 (25), 5129–5146 (2020). doi:10.1021/acs.jpca.0c02395

  • S. Venturi, M. P. Sharma, B. Lopez, M. Panesi, Data-Inspired and Physics-Driven Model Reduction for Dissociation: Application to the O2 + O System, J. Phys. Chem. A 124 (41), 8359–8372 (2020). doi:10.1021/acs.jpca.0c04516

  • R. L. Jaffe, D. W. Schwenke, M. Panesi, First Principles Calculation of Heavy Particle Rate Coefficients, Chapter 2 in Hypersonic Nonequilibrium Flows: Fundamentals and Recent Advances (AIAA, 2014).

Numerical methods

  • A. W. Sandvik, Numerical Solutions of the Schrödinger Equation, Boston University lecture notes (2016). Reference for the Numerov method used in level generation.

  • B. R. Johnson, The renormalized Numerov method applied to calculating bound states of the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation, J. Chem. Phys. 69, 4678 (1978). doi:10.1063/1.436421 — coupled-channel generalisation of the Numerov solver.

  • W. H. Press, S. A. Teukolsky, W. T. Vetterling, B. P. Flannery, Numerical Recipes: The Art of Scientific Computing (3rd ed., Cambridge University Press, 2007). Bulirsch–Stoer integrator and modified-midpoint method; see Trajectory Integrators.

  • L. Bonnet, J.-C. Rayez, Quasiclassical trajectory method for molecular scattering processes, Chem. Phys. Lett. 277, 183–190 (1997). doi:10.1016/S0009-2614(97)00881-6 — Gaussian-binning correction for final-state quantisation. Not yet implemented in CoarseAIR, tracked on Development Roadmap.

Potential energy surfaces

When citing CoarseAIR runs, please also cite the original PES paper for the surface actually used. The list below pairs each CoarseAIR PES model name with the paper that introduced it. Where we have a confirmed DOI it is linked inline; where not, please file a pull request / memo to docs/online-documentation/reference/bibliography.rst with the missing reference.

CNO system

The three low-lying electronic surfaces used by the CoarseAIR CNO system are the accurate DMBE potential energy surfaces of the Varandas / UCI-adjacent group:

  • CNO \({}^{2}A'\) (CNO_UCI_2A1) — Mota, Caridade, Varandas, Accurate DMBE potential energy surface for CNO(\({}^{2}A'\)), J. Phys. Chem. A 122 (23), 5173–5180 (2018). doi:10.1021/acs.jpca.8b01881

  • CNO \({}^{2}A''\) (CNO_UCI_2A2) — Mota, Varandas and co-workers, Accurate DMBE potential energy surface for CNO(\({}^{2}A''\)), J. Chem. Phys. 154, 034303 (2021). doi:10.1063/5.0039691

  • CNO \({}^{4}A''\) (CNO_UCI_4A2) — Varandas and co-workers, High-accuracy DMBE potential energy surface for \({}^{4}A''\) CNO, J. Chem. Phys. 159, 214307 (2023). doi:10.1063/5.0174547

Hydrogen-containing systems

  • H3 BKMP2 (H3_BH) — A. I. Boothroyd, W. J. Keogh, P. G. Martin, M. R. Peterson, A refined H:sub:`3` potential energy surface, J. Chem. Phys. 104, 7139 (1996).

  • H4 BMKP (H4_BMKP) — A. I. Boothroyd, P. G. Martin, W. J. Keogh, M. R. Peterson, An accurate analytic H:sub:`4` potential energy surface, J. Chem. Phys. 116, 666 (2002).

  • H2 + He BMP / BMPmod (H2He_BMPmod) — A. I. Boothroyd, P. G. Martin, M. R. Peterson, Accurate analytic He–H2 potential, J. Chem. Phys. 119, 3187 (2003).

Nitrogen systems

  • N3 NASA (N3_NASA) — M. Panesi, R. L. Jaffe, D. W. Schwenke, T. E. Magin, Rovibrational internal energy transfer and dissociation of N2 (\(^{1}\Sigma^{+}_{g}\)) + N(\(^{4}S\)) system in hypersonic flows, J. Chem. Phys. 138, 044312 (2013). doi:10.1063/1.4774412

  • N3 UMN / UMN_MBPIPMEG — Z. Varga, Y. Paukku, D. G. Truhlar and co-workers, N2–N triplet surfaces using Mixed Exponential Gaussian and permutation-invariant polynomial forms (University of Minnesota).

  • N4 NASA / UMN / UMN_PIPNN — UMN neural-network training of the four-body N2–N2 surface.

  • N2O Basel / UMN — Meuwly group (Basel) and Truhlar group (UMN) three-body surfaces for the Zel’dovich reaction.

Oxygen systems

  • O3 UMN — 9 electronic surfaces — Z. Varga, Y. Paukku, D. G. Truhlar, Potential energy surfaces for O + O2 collisions, series of papers (2017–2019) covering the 11A’, 11A’’, 3A’, 3A’’, 5A’, 5A’’, 21A’, 23A’, 25A’ manifold.

  • O3 NN / BNN / GP — neural-network, Bayesian-NN, and Gaussian-process regressions of the UMN ab-initio data, Venturi, Jaffe, Panesi (HERMES group, 2020 — see CoarseAIR foundations above).

  • O4 UMN — Paukku, Yang, Varga, Truhlar four-body O4 surface (UMN).

Carbon-containing systems

  • CO2 NASA / Basel — Schwenke and Jaffe (NASA Ames) and Meuwly (Basel) three-body surfaces for C + O2 / O + CO kinetics.

  • CO + Ar NASA — Schwenke and Jaffe non-reactive CO–Ar surface.

  • CHN UIUC / GUO — Illinois and Guo (UNM) surfaces for C + HN / H + CN / N + CH reactive kinetics.

  • NO2 UMN / Basel — UMN and Meuwly three-body surfaces for NO2 kinetics, with 22,4A’, 22,4A’’ manifold.

Internal documents shipping with the repository:

  • README/Documentation/CoarseAIR_Source_Code_Report.md — detailed source-code architecture report (March 2024).

Recommended reading

  • M. Karplus, R. N. Porter, R. D. Sharma, Exchange Reactions with Activation Energy. I. Simple Barrier Potential for (H, H2), J. Chem. Phys. 43, 3259 (1965). doi:10.1063/1.1697301 — the founding paper of the QCT method.

  • D. G. Truhlar, J. T. Muckerman, Reactive Scattering Cross Sections: Quasiclassical and Semiclassical Methods, in Atom–Molecule Collision Theory, R. B. Bernstein (Ed.), Plenum (1979) — standard textbook treatment.

  • R. E. Langer, On the Connection Formulas and the Solutions of the Wave Equation, Phys. Rev. 51, 669 (1937) — origin of the \(\ell(\ell+1) \to (\ell + \tfrac{1}{2})^{2}\) correction used throughout CoarseAIR.
