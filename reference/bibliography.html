Bibliography

Canonical references for the CoarseAIR methodology and for the most commonly used potential energy surfaces. Use these when citing CoarseAIR or any surface it ships.

CoarseAIR foundations

  • S. Venturi, Machine Learning and Uncertainty Quantification Framework for Predictive Ab Initio Hypersonics, Ph.D. thesis, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (2021).

  • S. Venturi, R. L. Jaffe, M. Panesi, Bayesian Machine Learning Approach to the Quantification of Uncertainties on Ab Initio Potential Energy Surfaces, J. Phys. Chem. A 124 (25), 5129–5146 (2020).

  • S. Venturi, M. P. Sharma, B. Lopez, M. Panesi, Data-Inspired and Physics-Driven Model Reduction for Dissociation: Application to the O2 + O System, J. Phys. Chem. A 124 (41), 8359–8372 (2020).

  • R. L. Jaffe, D. W. Schwenke, M. Panesi, First Principles Calculation of Heavy Particle Rate Coefficients, Chapter 2 in Hypersonic Nonequilibrium Flows: Fundamentals and Recent Advances (2014).

Numerical methods

  • A. W. Sandvik, Numerical Solutions of the Schrödinger Equation, Boston University lecture notes (2016). Reference for the Numerov method used in level generation.

  • B. R. Johnson, The renormalized Numerov method applied to calculating bound states of the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation, J. Chem. Phys. 69, 4678 (1978). Coupled-channel generalisation.

  • W. H. Press, S. A. Teukolsky, W. T. Vetterling, B. P. Flannery, Numerical Recipes: The Art of Scientific Computing (3rd ed., Cambridge University Press, 2007). Bulirsch–Stoer integrator and modified midpoint method; see Trajectory Integrators.

Potential energy surfaces

When citing CoarseAIR runs, please also cite the original PES reference for the surface actually used. A non-exhaustive list follows; for the full set, look at the headers of the individual source files under src/PESs/.

Hydrogen-containing systems

  • A. I. Boothroyd, W. J. Keogh, P. G. Martin, M. R. Peterson, J. Chem. Phys. 104, 7139 (1996). H3 BKMP2 surface (H3_BH).

  • A. I. Boothroyd, P. G. Martin, W. J. Keogh, M. R. Peterson, J. Chem. Phys. 116, 666 (2002). H4 BMKP surface (H4_BMKP).

  • A. I. Boothroyd, P. G. Martin, M. R. Peterson, J. Chem. Phys. 119, 3187 (2003). H2 + He (H2He_BMPmod).

Nitrogen systems

  • NASA N3, N4 surfaces: Jaffe and co-workers.

  • UMN N3 / N4 / N2O surfaces: Truhlar and co-workers (PIP, PIPNN, PIP-MEG forms).

Oxygen systems

  • UMN O3 surfaces (nine electronic states): Varga, Paukku, Truhlar.

  • UMN O4 surface: Paukku, Yang, Varga, Truhlar.

  • Varandas O2 diatomic: Varandas and co-workers.

Carbon-containing systems

  • NASA CO2 / CO-Ar surfaces: Schwenke, Jaffe.

  • Basel CNO / NO2 / CO2 / N2O surfaces: Meuwly and co-workers.

  • UCI CNO surfaces: University of California, Irvine group.

  • UIUC / GUO CHN surfaces: Illinois / Guo groups.

Source-code review

Internal documents shipping with the repository:

  • README/Documentation/CoarseAIR_User_s_Manual.pdf — the legacy user’s manual by S. Venturi and B. Lopez, documented by D. Steinberg.

  • README/Documentation/CoarseAIR_Source_Code_Report.md — detailed source-code architecture report (March 2024).

  • README/TheoryNotes/ — hand-written theory notes on coordinate transformations, sampling, trajectory analysis, and Maxwellian statistics.

Recommended reading

  • D. G. Truhlar, J. T. Muckerman, Reactive Scattering Cross Sections: Quasiclassical and Semiclassical Methods, in Atom–Molecule Collision Theory, R. B. Bernstein (Ed.), Plenum (1979).

  • L. Bonnet, J.-C. Rayez, Quasiclassical trajectory method for molecular scattering processes, Chem. Phys. Lett. 277, 183–190 (1997). Gaussian binning correction (not yet implemented in CoarseAIR).

  • M. Panesi, R. L. Jaffe, D. W. Schwenke, T. E. Magin, Rovibrational internal energy transfer and dissociation of N:sub:`2` (1Σ) + N(4S) system in hypersonic flows, J. Chem. Phys. 138, 044312 (2013). The N + N2 rate database that shaped the CoarseAIR design.
