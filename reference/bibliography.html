Bibliography

Canonical references for the CoarseAIR methodology and for the most commonly used potential energy surfaces. Use these when citing CoarseAIR or any surface it ships.

CoarseAIR foundations

  • S. Venturi, Machine Learning and Uncertainty Quantification Framework for Predictive Ab Initio Hypersonics, Ph.D. thesis, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (2021).

  • S. Venturi, R. L. Jaffe, M. Panesi, Bayesian Machine Learning Approach to the Quantification of Uncertainties on Ab Initio Potential Energy Surfaces, J. Phys. Chem. A 124 (25), 5129–5146 (2020). doi:10.1021/acs.jpca.0c02395

  • S. Venturi, M. P. Sharma, B. Lopez, M. Panesi, Data-Inspired and Physics-Driven Model Reduction for Dissociation: Application to the O2 + O System, J. Phys. Chem. A 124 (41), 8359–8372 (2020). doi:10.1021/acs.jpca.0c04516

  • R. L. Jaffe, D. W. Schwenke, M. Panesi, First Principles Calculation of Heavy Particle Rate Coefficients, Chapter 2 in Hypersonic Nonequilibrium Flows: Fundamentals and Recent Advances (AIAA, 2014).

Numerical methods

  • A. W. Sandvik, Numerical Solutions of the Schrödinger Equation, Boston University lecture notes (2016). Reference for the Numerov method used in level generation.

  • B. R. Johnson, The renormalized Numerov method applied to calculating bound states of the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation, J. Chem. Phys. 69, 4678 (1978). doi:10.1063/1.436421 — coupled-channel generalisation of the Numerov solver.

  • W. H. Press, S. A. Teukolsky, W. T. Vetterling, B. P. Flannery, Numerical Recipes: The Art of Scientific Computing (3rd ed., Cambridge University Press, 2007). Bulirsch–Stoer integrator and modified-midpoint method; see Trajectory Integrators.

  • L. Bonnet, J.-C. Rayez, Quasiclassical trajectory method for molecular scattering processes, Chem. Phys. Lett. 277, 183–190 (1997). doi:10.1016/S0009-2614(97)00881-6 — Gaussian-binning correction for final-state quantisation. Not yet implemented in CoarseAIR, tracked on /developer/roadmap.

Potential energy surfaces

When citing a CoarseAIR run, please also cite the paper that introduced the PES actually used. The catalogue below enumerates every concrete PES class currently compiled into CoarseAIR (src/PESs/*_PES_Class.F90), pairing the input-file model name with its underlying publication. Where a confirmed DOI has been established it is linked inline; where only a group / paper pattern is known the entry names the authors so the user can look up the specific paper, and notes that the PES parameter file header under dtb/Systems/<System>/<Model>/ carries the authoritative reference.

Generic (system-agnostic) frameworks

These classes provide the numerical scaffolding for ML-regressed surfaces and classical analytic forms; each chemical system supplies its own trained weights / parameters in dtb/Systems/<System>/.

Family

Reference

Venturi, Jaffe, Panesi (2020). See CoarseAIR foundations.

Class

System

Reference

N3_NASA

N + N2 (NASA Ames analytic)

D. W. Schwenke, R. L. Jaffe, M. Panesi — analytic fits used in Panesi, Jaffe, Schwenke, Magin, J. Chem. Phys. 138, 044312 (2013). doi:10.1063/1.4774412

N + N2 (UMN, MEG basis)

N4_NASA

D. W. Schwenke, R. L. Jaffe, M. Panesi — NASA analytic N4 surface used in hypersonic air-chemistry QCT campaigns.

N4_UMN

N2 + N2 (UMN, MEG basis)

Y. Paukku, K. R. Yang, Z. Varga, D. G. Truhlar, Global ab initio ground-state potential energy surface of N4, J. Chem. Phys. 139, 044309 (2013). doi:10.1063/1.4811653

N4_UMN_MBPIPMEG3

N2 + N2 (UMN, 3-body PIP-MEG decomposition)

Bender, Doraiswamy, Truhlar, Candler — PIP/MEG refinement of the UMN N4 PES. J. Chem. Phys. 140, 054302 (2014).

N4_UMN_PIPNN

N2 + N2 (UMN, PIP-NN)

Truhlar / Guo / Jiang groups — permutation-invariant neural network fit to the UMN N4 ab-initio points. See the dtb/Systems/N4/UMN_PIPNN/ header for the specific publication.

N2O_UMN

N2O (UMN triplets)

Lin, Varga, Truhlar and co-workers — N2O \({}^{3}A'\) / \({}^{3}A''\) analytic surfaces (UMN).

N2O_Basel

N2O (Basel)

Meuwly group (Basel) — three-body surface for N2 + O \(\leftrightarrow\) NO + N.

O3_UMN

O + O2 — 9 electronic surfaces (UMN)

Varga, Paukku, Truhlar and co-workers — analytic surfaces for the 11A’, 11A’’, 3A’, 3A’’, 5A’, 5A’’, 21A’, 23A’, 25A’ manifold. Cornerstone paper for the first six surfaces: Y. Paukku, Z. Varga, D. Venturi, Jaffe, Panesi (HERMES group) — NN trained on the UMN ab-initio points. See CoarseAIR foundations.

O4_UMN

O2 + O2 (UMN)

Y. Paukku, K. R. Yang, Z. Varga, A. W. Jasper, D. G. Truhlar, Global potential energy surface for the four-atom reactive system O4, J. Chem. Phys. 147, 034301 (2017). doi:10.1063/1.4994296

The BNN / GP variants of O3 share the About CoarseAIR-cited Venturi–Jaffe–Panesi (2020) publications; the input files select them by combining the generic frameworks (BNN, GP) with the O3 training-set parameters stored under dtb/Systems/O3/.

CNO (Breno Galvão / Varandas DMBE, combined-surface class)

Same three DMBE surfaces as CNO_UCI_2A1 / _2A2 / _4A2 (Mota & Varandas 2018; Mota et al. 2021; Varandas et al. 2023 — see below), implemented as a single legacy class for monolithic multi-surface runs. The three source PDFs are shipped under dtb/Systems/CNO/PESs/Breno/.

CO2_NASA

CO2 (C + O2 / O + CO NASA surfaces)

D. W. Schwenke, R. L. Jaffe — NASA Ames analytic CO2 ground-state surface for hypersonic-entry kinetics.

CO2_Basel

CO2 (Basel)

Meuwly and co-workers — Basel low-lying CO2 triplet surfaces (\({}^{3}A'\), \({}^{3}A''\)). Multiple papers 2017–2020; see dtb/Systems/CO2/Basel/ header.

COAr_NASA

CO + Ar (non-reactive NASA)

R. L. Jaffe and co-workers — NASA Ames three-body CO-Ar PES for rotational / vibrational relaxation studies.

NO2_UMN

NO2 (UMN)

Y. Paukku, Z. Varga, D. G. Truhlar — UMN \({}^{2}A'\) and \({}^{4}A'\) surfaces for NO + O \(\leftrightarrow\) O2 + N.

NO2_Basel

NO2 (Basel)

Meuwly group — three-body NO2 PES for the second Zel’dovich reaction.

For every entry not yet carrying an explicit DOI above, the authoritative citation is the header of the PES parameter file under dtb/Systems/<System>/<Model>/. Contributions that add the DOI to this catalogue via a pull request are welcome — the file to edit is docs/online-documentation/reference/bibliography.rst.

Recommended reading

  • M. Karplus, R. N. Porter, R. D. Sharma, Exchange Reactions with Activation Energy. I. Simple Barrier Potential for (H, H2), J. Chem. Phys. 43, 3259 (1965). doi:10.1063/1.1697301 — the founding paper of the QCT method.

  • D. G. Truhlar, J. T. Muckerman, Reactive Scattering Cross Sections: Quasiclassical and Semiclassical Methods, in Atom–Molecule Collision Theory, R. B. Bernstein (Ed.), Plenum (1979) — standard textbook treatment.

  • R. E. Langer, On the Connection Formulas and the Solutions of the Wave Equation, Phys. Rev. 51, 669 (1937) — origin of the \(\ell(\ell+1) \to (\ell + \tfrac{1}{2})^{2}\) correction used throughout CoarseAIR.
