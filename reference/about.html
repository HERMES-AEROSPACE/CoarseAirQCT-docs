About CoarseAIR

CoarseAIR is a Fortran 2008 object-oriented code for state-to-state and coarse-grained quasi-classical trajectory (QCT) simulations of atomic and molecular collisions on ab initio potential energy surfaces. It was originally developed to produce the state-resolved rate-coefficient databases needed for non-equilibrium aerothermodynamic simulations of hypersonic re-entry.

Authorship and license

  • Original author / copyright holder: Simone Venturi.

  • License: MIT (LICENSE file at the repository root).

  • Code base: derived from the VVTC (Vectorized Variable Stepsize Trajectory Code) of David W. Schwenke at NASA Ames Research Center, re-implemented in modern object-oriented Fortran.

  • Version: 1.1.

  • Repository: https://github.com/HERMES-AEROSPACE/CoarseAirQCT (private; development happens on the Clement-Adiabatic branch).

The code is currently maintained under the HERMES Aerospace research group led by Prof. Marco Panesi. Active contributors include Clement Civrais (ongoing extensions; lead of CoarseAIR+).

Related codes

CoarseAIR is one of three production codes shipped by the HERMES group; together they form a multiscale pipeline from ab-initio chemistry to hypersonic CFD:

  • CoarseAIR — produces state-resolved rate coefficients on ab-initio PES via QCT.

  • dmsAIR — direct molecular simulation (DSMC of state-resolved kinetics) that consumes CoarseAIR rate databases. https://hermes-aerospace.github.io/dmsAIR-docs/

  • PLATO — multi-temperature / state-to-state CFD solver that consumes the same chemistry databases.

A companion post-processing Python tool, PyCoarseAIR (https://github.com/simoneventuri/PyCoarseAIR), packages CoarseAIR output into the HDF5 format the downstream codes expect.

CoarseAIR+ (surface-hopping extension)

A separate fork of CoarseAIR — CoarseAIR+ — adds fewest-switches surface hopping (FSSH) for explicit electronic non-adiabatic transitions. The initial target system is ozone (O3) with its nine electronic surfaces; subsequent systems (CO2, NO2, CNO) follow. CoarseAIR+ shares the QCT machinery documented on this site; the surface-hopping layer is documented in a separate companion site.

Citation guidance

If you use CoarseAIR, please cite the references most relevant to your work — see Bibliography for the full list. The canonical entry points are:

  • S. Venturi, Machine Learning and Uncertainty Quantification Framework for Predictive Ab Initio Hypersonics, Ph.D. Thesis, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (2021).

  • S. Venturi, R. L. Jaffe, M. Panesi, Bayesian Machine Learning Approach to the Quantification of Uncertainties on Ab Initio Potential Energy Surfaces, J. Phys. Chem. A 124 (25), 5129–5146 (2020).

  • S. Venturi, M. P. Sharma, B. Lopez, M. Panesi, Data-Inspired and Physics-Driven Model Reduction for Dissociation: Application to the O:sub:`2` + O System, J. Phys. Chem. A 124 (41), 8359–8372 (2020).

If you use a specific PES (e.g. the UMN O3 surface, the NASA N4 surface), please also cite the original PES paper — see Bibliography.

Acknowledgements

The CoarseAIR code directly builds on the numerical methods and design choices of David Schwenke’s VVTC at NASA Ames, and incorporates PES implementations from the groups of Donald Truhlar (University of Minnesota), Markus Meuwly (Basel), Hua Guo (University of New Mexico), Richard Jaffe (NASA Ames), and Bruno Silva (Aveiro), among others.

The documentation site you are reading was assembled by Clement Civrais in the HERMES Aerospace group, with sources in docs/online-documentation/.

Contact

Questions and bug reports: open an issue at https://github.com/HERMES-AEROSPACE/CoarseAirQCT/issues — ideally with a reproducible minimum input and the output of coarseair-initialize.x.
